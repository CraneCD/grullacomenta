Throughout the journey of One Piece, many stories are told that make us think from the deepest part of our being, and even cause us to reevaluate ourselves on a personal level. But in my opinion, of everything that has been told in the anime so far, no story comes close to the impact of the evolution of our favorite Straw Hat chef, Black Leg Sanji.

Not everything about Sanji's character is perfect. Oda has given him certain personality traits that neither I nor many fans agree with, particularly his unbridled perversion, which at times even contradicts a part of his personality, the kind of knight in shining armor he is with women. However, I would venture to say that this is not his main struggle in psychological terms, but rather an entirely secondary one. Sanji's central struggle is even more fundamental, and it is rooted in the nature of his family and his childhood: what do you do when everyone around you is inhuman and you are the only human with emotions? And what would happen if, over time, you saw the possibility of becoming someone inhuman yourself, and how would you deal with that?

Sanji is one of the characters who has received the most flashbacks in the work, not only with his original story alongside Zeff, surviving on the island with no food, but also with the entire story of Germa and of his family as the children of the mad scientist Judge. To recall the context: Germa 66 is a group of villains who literally stepped out of a cartoon, using technology to clone superhumans and create cyborg children with superhuman abilities and no emotions. Judge managed to turn Germa into the largest group of military mercenaries in the world of One Piece, single-handedly winning countless wars in the North Blue. All of Judge's children, from Yonji on down, are superhumans capable of doing anything, since they lack any kind of empathy. Well, perhaps there is one small exception. But do you know who is not just an exception, but someone who goes completely against everything that Ichiji, Yonji, and even Judge himself represent? Sanji.

Sanji, the third son of Judge and Sora, was born without the superhuman abilities of his brothers, and on top of that, he was born the most human of the entire family, having empathy even for the smallest animals. He grew up watching how the normal thing around him was to be useless for not being like his brothers, yet somehow instinctively understanding the difference between good and evil, and deciding for himself, despite all the influence of his father and his own brothers, to do good. A large part of this came from the influence of his mother, who, despite being with Judge, also seems to be an empathetic and sensitive person, and who surely taught Sanji the difference between what the rest of his family does and the broader morality of the world he lives in.

That is surely the origin of two key aspects of Sanji. One: his absolute empathy, being the only ray of sunlight in a world as dark and devastating as Germa. And two: his unconditional love and devotion to women, since the female gender was the only one that showed him appreciation and taught him that he was not completely useless. The love given to him by his mother Sora and by his sister Reiju at different moments in his life taught him that women are to be admired and protected, and that under no circumstances should he attack them or fight against them.

Sanji grew up seeing the rest of his family as inhuman monsters who tortured everyone around them, even him, for lacking the abilities and mindset of his brothers. To Judge, Sanji is trash, and to Sanji, Judge and his experiments are subhuman, complete demons. This defines his character as someone who not only seeks humanity through his demonstrated empathy, shown when he grows up by his complete objectivity in everything that involves feeding someone, no matter whether they are good or evil, but who also seeks humanity as an absolute rejection of his birth family, going so far as to discard the Vinsmoke surname and to consider his adoptive father his only family. In the area where Sanji shows his love, the kitchen, Zeff was not just someone who taught him to cook, but an adoptive father who reinforced the main characteristics that Sanji demonstrates throughout the story: loyalty, empathy, calm, and chivalry. I think it is fair to say that without Zeff's influence, Sanji probably would not be the person he is today.

Throughout One Piece, Sanji has demonstrated a capacity to observe situations and to act in an intelligent and measured way in order to gain information or save his friends. Two perfect examples of this are when he had the conversation with Crocodile, extracting information from him, and when he infiltrated the train on the way to Enies Lobby, predicting that something was wrong with Robin. But it is only in the second part of the story, after the time skip, after Marineford, that we see the true depth of Sanji's humanity, and how at times he wavered between having to adopt his inhuman side, granted to him at birth by Judge's experiments. Specifically, the Whole Cake Island and Wano arcs show us this.

In Whole Cake, Sanji finds himself in a contradiction of his principles. On one hand, abandoning the Straw Hats, marrying Big Mom's daughter Pudding, and returning in a certain way to the wars of his family is the only way he sees to save the most important people in his life, giving himself up completely and selflessly. But on the other hand, this forces him to act like an inhuman being who does not think about the real feelings of his captain and his nakama. Nothing demonstrates this better than the fight between Sanji and Luffy, where Sanji shows his conviction to the point of attacking Luffy with all his strength to force him to stop chasing him, begging him in order to save him. This stubbornness is a demonstration of Sanji's distrust in the abilities of Luffy and the rest to find an alternative to solve the current problem, and it contradicts Sanji's apparent inhumanity with his real, human intentions. It is only when Pudding reveals her true self and Sanji's heart is completely broken, and faced with the complete confidence in Sanji's humanity that his captain Luffy holds despite the beating Sanji gave him, that he finally makes the decision to reject the path painted for him by his family and chooses to leave with his new family, his Straw Hats.

However, even after Whole Cake Island, there are residues of Germa's influence in Sanji's mind, and there is no better reminder of this than his Raid Suit, a tool of war created by Germa specifically for him, which ironically gives him the one power he has truly desired: to become invisible, even if it is for the reasons we like least about his character. This Power Ranger suit, and his ability to use it to become more effective, creates the same dilemma he lived through on Whole Cake Island. Yes, it is a reminder of the lack of humanity of his birth family, but at the same time it is a way to become stronger in order to defeat opponents who could threaten his new family. That is why Sanji does not discard it immediately, despite condemning what Germa does verbally.

It is in the fight against Queen in the middle of Onigashima that Sanji manages to break the barrier of his indecision between the human and the inhuman, and is truly free to make a place for himself among the wings of the Pirate King. In this fight, Queen is a reminder of Judge, being part of the same scientific group as him, and again and again he insists and pressures Sanji to show him the powers of the Raid Suit. Sanji refuses, again and again. But despite his rejection of the suit, his body begins to change, starting by showing the same physical improvements as the brothers he has always criticized: a hard, resistant body and the ability to recover his original form, giving him basically super endurance and resilience. Sanji immediately realizes that becoming an inhuman being is probably not something that is 100% within his control; it may be mere genetics. His desperation and confusion do not allow him to fight with all his strength against Queen, who represents that very influence causing the changes in his body. It even reaches the point where we are shown Queen's representation of what Germa is, giving them the powers of his brothers: he has the electric powers of Niji, he has the laser eyes that Ichiji has, and he has the kind of claw and superstrength that Yonji has, all the superpowers of his brothers.

It is only when Sanji, remembering the influence of his mother and his true nature and responsibility to his captain, who not only saved him multiple times from despair but also gave him a new family that understood and accepted him for who he is, that he manages to make a true decision, a profound decision rooted in the deepest part of his being: to destroy the Raid Suit, the reminder and his bond to Germa, and ironically to accept his inhuman future for the sake of the human he has to be in the present. Sanji decides to use the improvements in his body to create a hotter flame in the form of Ifrit, and even reaches the point of asking his rival Zoro for help so that he can fight with peace of mind, giving Zoro the trust to finish him off if at any moment he loses control. Sanji rejects inhumanity by accepting it, and in that moment he is free and reaches his true level of power, which allows him to defeat someone like Queen.

Sanji's very existence is a contradiction in itself. His birth and evolution owe a great deal to the genetics and inhuman science of Judge and Germa, yet at the same time he is one of the most human and empathetic people in all of One Piece, capable of even accepting the inhumanity he so abhors in order to keep his convictions and be the guardian angel that protects the entire crew. It is something similar to what Zeff did to save him when he was on that deserted island: he demonstrated a complete capacity to give himself up in order to save Sanji, a person he did not even appreciate that much. Sanji seems to be making a similar sacrifice, but with completely different motivations. In this case, what he wants to do is save the crew that has become his new family. A point we cannot forget is that Sanji even reaches the point of asking the last person he would ever want to ask for help, showing a humility that an inhuman being could never possess. And in the end, it all demonstrates that Black Leg Sanji has rejected the inhuman and is the most human of all of One Piece.
