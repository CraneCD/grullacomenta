In Kimetsu no Yaiba, there are interconnections between all the characters, whether they're protagonists, antagonists, or even side characters, and the beautiful thing about the story is the sense of inherited destiny it conveys through its characters. However, there is one little girl who not only represents everything Kimetsu wants to show us, but who is, all on her own, the most important character in the entire series, not just for the side of the hunters but also for the side of the demons. Of course, she is none other than Nezuko Kamado, Tanjiro's little demon sister.

Nezuko is a crucial character in Kimetsu no Yaiba. Her mere existence is a threat to both humans and demons due to the unpredictability of her demonic powers. Although she has a miraculous control over her bloodlust, many in the Demon Slayer Corps don't believe she should exist. Nonetheless, with Tanjiro's help, Nezuko is allowed to accompany him and others in search of a cure. It's suggested that Nezuko's transition into a demon was never fully completed, hence the room for her to return. Even so, Nezuko's state allows her to transform and gradually evolve, and she does so on several occasions during battles to protect Tanjiro and the rest of her protectors.

As a recipient of the first progenitor's blood, Nezuko was granted demonic powers without remaining permanently in that state. For some inexplicable reason, she developed an unprecedented resistance to human blood and sunlight. However, it seems that most of her resistance comes from her strong sense of protecting Tanjiro. Her brother is mainly there to calm her down whenever she's on the verge of losing control. What we've witnessed since the first season is Nezuko's "dormant" or "hybrid" transformation, where most of her humanity is active. But in the second season, Nezuko finally awakened to her true demonic form, the "Awakened" form. This form is considered the maximum level Nezuko can reach in her demonic powers so far, when she enters an unrestrained state, leaving all of her humanity behind in the process. Her Awakened form is different because the transformation is more complete, making her even more powerful and highly aggressive.

Despite not being a member of the Twelve Kizuki, Nezuko is a very powerful demon. Her most outstanding trait is her extraordinary growth rate, due to the large amount of blood she received from Muzan Kibutsuji when he turned her into a demon. This has been demonstrated in many battles where she was able to defeat her enemies despite being overwhelmed by them at first. She has been shown to defeat her enemies by increasing her own physical capabilities, enhancing her regeneration, or developing her own Blood Demon Art in critical situations.

Nezuko has demonstrated great skill and prowess in combat, surpassing the Temple Demon, the Swamp Demon, and Susamaru. Later on, her power increased even further to the point where she could face members of the Twelve Kizuki, such as Daki, whom she managed to defeat easily despite not initially being a match for her, and Hantengu's clones, with whom she was able to fight on equal terms.

Nezuko has several unique characteristics that set her apart from all other demons. The most notable is that Nezuko can continuously restore her power without consuming human blood, as any other demon would need to do. Instead, she recovers her strength only through sleep, although she would have to spend a long time sleeping in order to recover and regain her power. In addition, Nezuko has an extraordinary growth rate, as has been shown several times when she becomes stronger without any training or consumption of humans. This ability is shown even in the middle of battle and with great increases in power.

Nezuko has the ability to enter a berserker state where she becomes more demonic in appearance, transforming into an adult version of herself with vine-like tattoos surrounding her body and a single horn protruding from the right side of her forehead. Nezuko's physical capabilities in this state increase drastically to the point where she could sever Daki's limbs by stomping on them and kick her from extremely far distances, destroying multiple buildings in the process. Her regenerative abilities also become equal to those of the Upper Ranks of the Twelve Kizuki. When she first awakened this ability in her fight with Daki, she became frenzied in battle, lost her usual restraint toward human blood, and only returned to normal when Tanjiro sang her a lullaby. Over time, she was able to transform into her Advanced Demon Form while maintaining her usual composure and avoiding feeding on or attacking humans.

We can't forget the fact that, like all demons, Nezuko was initially vulnerable to sunlight, and her body would completely disintegrate if exposed to it. As a result, she could only go out at night or in areas with little sunlight. However, in an event that completely changed the order of the war between hunters and demons, during her battle with Hantengu, she suddenly managed to develop immunity to sunlight, which allowed her to be active regardless of whether she was in the sun or not, and earned her the title of "The Chosen Demon."

All of this makes Nezuko completely unique compared to the rest of the demons created by Muzan, and it's what makes her the key piece for ending the series' conflict. However, we can't overlook her special ability.

Blood Demon Art: Exploding Blood

Nezuko's Blood Demon Art allows her to ignite her blood in pink-colored flames at will. Her flames have a special property that makes them harmful only to demons, objects belonging to demons, and the Blood Demon Arts of other demons, while being completely harmless to humans and other objects. These flames can hinder natural demonic regeneration, as was shown when Daki's burns took much longer to heal than traditional wounds. This technique was also able to neutralize Gyutaro's poison to save Inosuke and Tengen. Her flames also have the ability to temporarily turn nichirin swords a glowing red, increasing the strength of the attacks inflicted by them. This technique, mixed with the hunters' breathing styles, can be a weaker replacement for the red nichirin swords needed to finish off the demon king, and it seems specially designed to finish off the kizuki. However, this Blood Demon Art doesn't come without drawbacks, since because Nezuko doesn't consume human blood and depends on the rest of sleep, extended use of her technique causes her to become fatigued quickly, to the point of falling asleep.

In addition to her blood art, Nezuko has shown in her Advanced Demon Form that she has control over the viscosity of her own blood. When her limbs and head were severed and she was about to be attacked by Daki, Nezuko managed to control her limbs and head by coagulating and solidifying the blood gushing from them before the streams could break. She was able to command her limbs to grab and stop Daki's bands, and even though she did so with floating, severed limbs connected by blood, she was able to do it with such force that the bands came to a dead stop. She was then able to bring all her limbs together through those streams of blood, allowing her body to regenerate.

In conclusion, although Nezuko is clearly a relatively weak demon when it comes to the higher tiers of power on the demons' side, her particular abilities make her the key piece for the resolution and evolution not only of the plot but of the main characters on both sides in Kimetsu no Yaiba. Her blood and her very being are both the main motivator for Tanjiro and Muzan and the mechanism by which there exists a hope for stopping the demons, as well as the necessary complement for creating the perfect demon, making her, in my opinion, one of the most interesting characters in the entire work.
