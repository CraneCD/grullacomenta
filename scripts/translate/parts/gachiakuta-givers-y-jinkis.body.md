---
The depth of the soul is not measured by what we hoard behind glass display cases, but by what we rescue from oblivion. In the ecosystem of _Gachiakuta_, the first season throws an uncomfortable truth in our face: we are what we throw away. But for the **Givers**, trash is not the end of a cycle, but the womb of a new existence. It is that spark of affection, that "respect for the object," that separates a mere collector from an artist of combat.

While everything may seem like trash in this world, the universe of the Cleaners and the residents of the Abyss is denser than it appears. Each Giver who appears in this initial stage of the story brings a distinct shade to the philosophy of "recovered value." It is not just about hitting hard; it is about how your psyche intertwines with inanimate matter to give it a purpose. Let us continue this descent into the dump to explain all the Givers and their Jinki from this first installment of the anime.


---
## Enjin: The Pillar of Connection and the "Umbreaker"

Enjin is the chassis that keeps everything from falling apart among the Cleaners. At 28, he is the mentor that the protagonist—and honestly all of us—needed. His name, which according to him comes from an ancient language, means "the person who connects others" (縁人), a perfect description for a leader who rescues broken souls from the dump.

### Umbreaker: The Shelter That Becomes a Drill

His Jinki, Umbreaker, is a worn-out umbrella. In a world where rain is a constant reminder of the Ground's toxicity, an umbrella is a symbol of absolute protection. However, Enjin transforms it into a tool of absurd versatility:

- **Defense and Flight:** Open, Umbreaker offers a solid defense and lets Enjin maneuver through the air, almost like a punk, rough-edged version of Mary Poppins.
 
- **Piercing Attack:** Closed, the Jinki becomes a drill spinning at extreme speeds, capable of shredding the armor of Trash Beasts as if it were paper.
 
- **Octa-Shredder:** A devastating technique where Enjin falls from a great height, unleashing a massive impact that can clear out multiple enemies in a single blow.
 
- **Long-Range Modification:** By using a cable, Enjin can throw his umbrella while keeping his distance, proving that his style is not just brute force, but tactical analysis.
 

Enjin is an enigma. Although his name is engraved on his Jinki—suggesting he uses it at full power—there is a constant suspicion that he is hiding something more. He is a pacifist who forbids his subordinates from killing humans, but who does not hesitate to use Rudo as bait if it guarantees a lesson in survival.

## Zanka Nijiku: The Pride of the "Average Man"

Zanka is the perfect example that genetic talent is nothing against pure obsession. Coming from the noble Nijiku family, destined to be Hell Guards (Guardians of Hell), Zanka grew up surrounded by "geniuses" who made him feel like trash. After being humiliated by Hyo, he locked himself in a pit for three days until Enjin found him clinging to a simple wooden stick.

His Jinki is called Lovely Assistaff (or Aibo, a play on words between "companion" and "love staff"). What was once a stick discarded by mistake is now a weapon of surgical precision:

- **Sasumata Form:** When activated, the tip becomes a restraining tool (sasumata) with blades and points that can impale and restrain enemies.
 
- **Vibration Detection:** By driving the staff into the ground, Zanka can feel vibrations in the area, allowing him to locate enemies even if his vision is blocked.
 
- **Extreme Mobility:** Zanka uses it to propel himself and perform chain attacks at supersonic speeds, a product of his hellish training with his sister Kyoka.
 

Zanka calls himself an "average loser," but it is that very insecurity that drives him to be the best at wielding vital instruments. His Jinki reflects his desire to be "useful" (Assist), but on his own "original" terms.

## Riyo Reaper: The Dance of the Assassin and "The Ripper"

Riyo is a bubbly chaos who hides a past stained with blood. At 16 and with inexhaustible energy, she was a hired killer before Enjin convinced her to change her life. Her design, nicknamed by some as a "Jet Set Radio punk," is deceptive; beneath her cheerfulness lies a lethal pragmatism.

### The Ripper: Scissors That Cut Destiny

Her Jinki, The Ripper, is a pair of gigantic scissors that she wields in a way that defies human anatomy: with her feet.

- **Acrobatic Style:** Riyo uses martial arts and acrobatic movements to swing the scissors with her legs, creating massive cutting arcs.
 
- **Size Manipulation:** The scissors can change size to adapt to combat, allowing her to cut Trash Beasts the size of buildings.
 
- **Deflation Effect:** One of her strangest abilities is her capacity to "deflate" Rudo when he swells from an excess of Anima.
 
- **Stealth and Efficiency:** As a former hitwoman, perhaps her Jinki is not her most lethal weapon, since she literally has a gun stashed in her pocket, which we have seen her use with incredible effectiveness, despite Enjin's restrictions.
## 4. Eishia Stilza

Eishia is an important exception in the world of the Cleaners. She is, possibly, the most indispensable and humanitarian member of Janem. In a narrative saturated with "destruction with purpose," Eishia represents the "maintenance of existence." Her design, with those cables that look like external veins, takes on a much deeper meaning when we understand that her function is not to take life, but to keep it from slipping away through the cracks of the Abyss.

- **The Jinki (Vital Instrument):** A power cable that, far from being a conductor of death, acts as an external life-support system. It is the physical manifestation of technical empathy. While others use their Jinki to turn trash into weapons, Eishia uses hers to turn the soul's energy into medicine.
 
- **Character Analysis:** Eishia embodies silent resilience. Her role as a "healer" places her in a position of unique vulnerability and power. She is not a front-line fighter, but she is the one who allows the front line to keep existing. Her psychology must be that of someone with an iron will; seeing the constant damage her comrades suffer and having the ability—and the burden—to reverse it requires an emotional stability that few possess. She is the reminder that, even in the deepest dump in the world, caring for others remains a priority.
 
- **The Bond:** Her Jinki is the extension of her own nervous system connecting to that of others. When Eishia uses her cable to heal, she is not just passing along electricity; she is sharing her vitality to patch up that of others. Her Jinki symbolizes interconnection: no one survives alone in the Abyss. The power cable becomes an umbilical cord that binds the team, allowing the transfer of "charge" to close wounds and restore functions.

## Tamsy Caines: The Tension of the Cord and the Hyo-Hyo

If most Givers find a purpose of redemption in their Jinki, Tamsy finds a tool of manipulation. He is a character who operates on the margins, whose true face is revealed only when the knots are tight enough.

- **The Jinki (Tokushin):** A distaff that generates and manipulates thread of an absurd resilience. It is not for "fixing" things, it is for **entangling**. His ability to deploy these threads allows him to create an invisible web of control over the battlefield, in addition to tightening them to leave his opponents immobile and even without aura, and unraveling it to create an incredibly powerful area attack, pinning his opponents to the floor.
 
- **Character Analysis:** Tamsy represents the "surface tension" of loyalty. As an infiltrator of the Vandals, his role as support for the Cleaners was the perfect cover. While everyone thought he was "weaving" safety for the team, he was actually preparing the net to hunt them. His psychology is that of a patient predator; he does not need brute force because he knows that a well-placed thread can bring down a giant.
 
- **The Bond:** The use of a distaff is deeply symbolic. It represents the control of destiny. Tamsy feels superior because he believes he holds everyone's strings in his hands. His bond with Tokushin is not born of the desire to help, but of the pleasure of restraint. To him, others are simple fibers he can twist at will. The revelation of his betrayal gives Tokushin a macabre air: every cord he threw to "save" someone was, in reality, a shackle in potential.

## Delmon Gates: The Hydration of Rage and the Farmer's Dream

At the opposite end of Team Eager's moral spectrum is Delmon Gates. Delmon is the antithesis of subtlety; he is a loud, passionate, and deeply emotional man whose strength comes from a genuine love and a devastating loss.

Delmon's Jinki is a water hose, an object that in the context of the Ground is a luxury and a survival tool. Originally called (Thirst Extinguisher)

Delmon was not always a warrior. He was a farmer who worked tirelessly to fulfill the dream of his late wife: to see a flower bloom in the toxic desert of the Ground. The phrase "Grow big!" was his mantra as he watered his plants, and it is that will to foster life that awakened his Jinki. However, on the battlefield, this intention to "make things grow" turns lethal. Delmon can control the water content of any target that absorbs it. When he fires at his enemies, they swell up like balloons until the internal pressure causes them to explode.

Delmon's use of his hose is highly technical, employing different nozzles for various tactical effects:

- **Silent Nozzle:** Emits a fine spray that incapacitates targets without causing a racket, leaving them unconscious almost instantly.
 
- **High Pressure Nozzle:** Launches jets of water at speeds so extreme they can punch through concrete walls and dismember Trash Beasts.

Delmon's loyalty to the Cleaners is unbreakable because he sees in the organization the only way to protect what little beauty remains in the world. His hatred of the Raiders is visceral, since they represent the destructive force that ruined his farm and, by extension, his wife's legacy. Despite his "gentle giant" appearance, Delmon is capable of a blind fury if his comrades are threatened, which makes him the "emotional muscle" of the team.

## Bro Santa: The Supervisor of Peace and the Tension of the Adoptive Family

The structure of the Cleaners includes units like Team Child, designed to handle younger Givers or those with abilities that require specific supervision. At the head of this team is Bro Santa, a 35-year-old man who personifies paternal responsibility in an environment where childhood is an almost nonexistent concept.

### Cloth (Cross): The Headband as a Bond of Unity

Bro's Jinki is a piece of cloth that he uses as a hairband, called Cloth (or Cross in some translations due to Japanese phonetics). This object is an Attacker-type Jinki characterized by its superhuman elasticity and resilience.

When Bro activates his Jinki, the cloth extends massively and his name appears engraved on it in the language of the Abyss, which shows that Bro operates at his maximum potential without the reservations that Tamsy displays. Its functions include:

- **Restraint and Lashing:** He can use the cloth as a whip to strike or as cords to immobilize multiple enemies simultaneously.
 
- **Tactical Mobility:** Bro moves through the environment using the cloth as a grappling hook, which compensates for his great size (195 cm) and allows him to maneuver with surprising agility.
 
- **Team Support:** One of its noblest applications is using the cloth to grab and catapult his allies, improving the reach of Dear's or Rudo's attacks.
 

### The Philosophy of the Supervisor and the Raising of a Giver

Bro is a firm advocate of peace and often apologizes for Dear's erratic actions, recognizing that children process the world differently. His relationship with Dear is that of an adoptive father who understands that a child's Jinki is, often, their only defense mechanism against a cruel world. Bro believes that once a child sets their mind to something, they can achieve incredible things, and his role is not to limit them, but to guide them so they do not lose their way in the process. It is notable that Bro hates sweets, an ironic contrast with his name and his role as "Santa," suggesting a maturity that rejects the superficial.

## Dear Santa: The Roar of Silence and the Paradox of Childhood

Dear Santa is, at 10 years old, one of the most feared combatants among the Cleaners. His design has been described by fans as a "Rugrats punk," with his two-tone hair styled into eight spiky tails and his oversized blue overalls. What defines Dear is his almost absolute silence and his dependence on a pacifier that is, at once, his greatest weakness and his source of power.

### Centralian: The Pacifier That Drains Existence

Dear's Jinki, Centralian, is his pacifier. In the context of a Giver, a comfort object like a pacifier suggests that Dear became a Giver in a moment of extreme vulnerability, when he needed to "cling" to something to survive emotionally.

Centralian possesses one of the most dangerous abilities in the Cleaners' arsenal: Power Drain.

- **Gathering Steam:** Dear starts every fight with the physical strength of a 10-year-old child, which is insignificant. However, with each blow he lands, his Jinki absorbs the target's energy.
 
- **Progressive Strengthening:** As he absorbs power, Dear's strength, speed, and endurance increase exponentially. A prolonged fight against Dear is a death sentence, since he grows stronger while his opponent weakens.
 
- **Comfort Sensory:** It has been theorized that the constant use of the pacifier is a necessity for Dear, allowing him to manage the sensory stress of battles and his own irritable nature.
 

### The Non-Verbal Warrior and the Connection to "Thought"

Dear only speaks to pronounce the name of his Jinki, which suggests that his Anima is so concentrated on the object that conventional language has become unnecessary for him. His temperament is volatile; he is prone to losing his patience quickly, especially if he is treated like a baby or denied his favorite snacks. His relationship with Bro is the only anchor that keeps him within the limits of civilization, and his rivalry with Rudo shows that, despite his immense power, he is still a child seeking validation and snacks.


## Guita Hebby Fantasia: The Fury of Plush

Guita is, possibly, the most "unsettling" member of Team Child. With a design that evokes a Splatoon character or a plush nightmare, this cyan-haired girl is sweet but completely deranged.

### Guita: The Onesie That Is an Organic Mecha

Her Jinki is her own romper, or Kaiju "onesie." Guita does not fight with external weapons; she becomes the weapon.

- **Kaiju Transformation:** When activated, her Jinki grows massively until it turns her into a giant monster capable of trampling Trash Beasts that dwarf the other Cleaners.
 
- **Plush Command Cockpit:** Curiously, Guita's body does not grow; she remains small inside the monster's mouth or head, "piloting" her Jinki as if it were a plush mecha.
 
- **Berserker Personality:** Despite her adorable appearance, in her Kaiju form she is a force of pure destruction, often limited only by her own erratic behavior or lack of attention.

## Semiu Grier: The Gaze That Precedes Destiny

Semiu is not just the receptionist of the Cleaners; she is the leader of Team Front and, possibly, the person with the clearest vision in all of the Ground. At 26, Semiu personifies absolute professionalism, although her hobby of reading adult magazines during office hours reminds us that no one is perfect.

### Eyes: The Glasses of Tactical Clairvoyance

Her Jinki, appropriately called Eyes, consists of her glasses. Unlike other Jinki, Eyes possesses no direct offensive capabilities against Trash Beasts, which places Semiu in a role of elite support and base defense.

- **Combat Clairvoyance:** Semiu can see brief fragments of the immediate future, which allows her to dodge bullets and projectiles with an elegance that borders on the supernatural.
 
- **Perception of Essence:** By concentrating, she can see the true nature or "essence" of a person, detecting any anomaly in their Anima.
 
- **The Price of Truth:** Using Eyes carries an extreme physical risk. If Semiu removes her limiters to fully synchronize with her Jinki, the recoil could leave her blind forever.
 

Semiu prefers office work not out of weakness, but because she enjoys the aesthetic of the "office lady," even though she is capable of knocking out a squad of looters all by herself.

## Remlin Tysark: The Spark of Succession

Lastly, let us talk about an honorary Cleaner. Remlin is the current Spellcaster of Canvas City, succeeding the deceased Gob. At 14, Remlin is a whirlwind of hyperactive, androgynous energy who understands that art is not just aesthetics, but a form of spiritual warfare.

### The Pen: A Legacy of Ink and Thought

Their Jinki is a fountain pen inherited from Gob. It is one of the rare cases of a "Succession" Jinki, which means it has accumulated the Anima of multiple generations of artists, making Remlin a "Natural Giver."

- **Art-Magic:** Remlin draws spells that materialize their feelings or intentions on any surface.
 
- **Protective Enchantments:** They can imbue objects or people with "protective spells" that reduce damage taken, even from blows that would be fatal.
 
- **Ephemeral Creations:** Remlin has proven capable of transforming aerosol cans into infinite fireworks or creating gigantic ink monsters.
 

Remlin lives by the motto of creative freedom; if it were up to them, everyone in the Ground would wear tattoos. Their ability to emotionally bounce back from the tragedy of losing their mentor into an explosive joy is what fuels the power of their strokes.

## Rudo Surebrec: The Vandal of Trash and the Touch of the Watchman

Lastly, and perhaps most importantly, Rudo Surebrec, our protagonist, is the epicenter of the storm. At 15, his life in the Sphere was an exercise in survival and ostracism due to his origin as "Tribesfolk." Falsely accused of the murder of his father figure, Regto, and thrown into the Abyss, Rudo did not fall alone: he fell with a visceral hatred and a pair of gloves that would change the rules of the Ground.

### 3R: The Gloves That Grant Purpose to the Useless

Rudo's Jinki, called 3R, belongs to the mythical Watchman Series. In the first season of the anime, it is established that its power is tied to the sense of touch. Unlike other Givers who bond to a single object, Rudo is an anomaly: he can turn practically any piece of trash into a temporary Jinki simply by touching it.

- **Versatility of Discards:** Rudo has transformed everything from broken keyboards to nail guns into lethal weapons. However, these objects tend to disintegrate after fulfilling their function.
 
- **Concentration of Anima:** His most technical ability allows him to focus the Anima of three different objects into a single one, multiplying its power by two or three (x2/x3) and altering its physical form for devastating attacks.
 
- **Appetite for Sweets and Fury:** Rudo is a protagonist driven by rage, but with a comic weakness for sweets. His combat style is raw and direct, reflecting his initial inability to process emotions other than revenge.
 

Rudo represents the concept that nothing is truly trash if there is someone capable of seeing its hidden value. He is the "Natural Giver" par excellence, although his emotional instability is the greatest obstacle to mastering his potential.

In the end, the analysis of these Givers forces us to reconsider our relationship with the everyday. In _Gachiakuta_, an umbrella, a pair of office glasses, or a plush pajama cease to be objects and become mirrors of the soul. Even secondary characters teach us a great deal: Semiu teaches us that seeing the truth comes at a price; Guita shows us that our childhood fascination can be our greatest weapon; and Remlin reminds us that art is the only legacy that can truly survive death. "Thought" is the only currency that really matters in the Ground, and as long as there are people willing to care for what others discard, humanity will have a chance against the abyss of the Sphere.
