Cursed techniques in Jujutsu Kaisen have always been an expression of their author's imagination, a way of giving different and creative powers to his characters, but some techniques are more creative than others, and none represents this concept more than the innate cursed technique of not one but two characters in the series, Mai and Yorozu, who share the difficult-to-use technique of Construction.

Construction, as its name implies, allows the user to conjure an object out of nothing. Unlike objects created inside a domain, anything constructed will remain even after the technique is finished. However, this uses a large amount of cursed energy inefficiently, which can cause a strong adverse reaction in the user's body.

Mai

Mai is Mai's twin, whom we meet briefly during the competition between the Jujutsu schools of Tokyo and Kyoto,

Like her twin sister, Mai was mistreated by the Zenin clan while growing up, simply for being a girl with little talent for jujutsu. Mai was content to live under their control as a servant and preferred doing household chores rather than fighting. Maki, on the other hand, preferred to defy the clan and work as a sorcerer. The repercussions of Maki's rebellion forced Mai to study to become a jujutsu sorcerer. Because of this, Mai detests the grotesque work of exorcising curses and resents Maki for essentially throwing them both into that life.

Mai developed the Construction technique and hid it from her twin sister while they were growing up. She uses a revolver in battle, so after firing six shots at Maki, it seemed she had run out of bullets. However, Construction allowed her to create and load another bullet charged with cursed energy into her revolver. This surprise attack was meant to catch her opponent off guard and end the fight.

Mai's limit is one bullet per day, but this drained the little cursed energy she possessed, resulting in physical consequences such as a nosebleed. After being cut down by her own father, Mai decided to use Construction to give her sister Maki a fighting chance. Mai was aware that the repercussions would kill her, but she seized that opportunity to take all the cursed energy between the twins. Mai sacrificed her life to give Maki a body of steel that escaped cursed energy, using Construction to create a great sword that symbolizes the bond between the two.

Sadly, Mai is more of a vehicle to empower Maki and doesn't have great feats or much to mention when it comes to Construction. For that, we have to talk about the other exponent of the technique.

Yorozu

Yorozu is one of the sorcerers reincarnated during Kenjaku's Culling Game, who took over the body of Tsumiki Fushiguro in her new life. In the Heian era, Yorozu was considered an extremely talented sorcerer, even recognized by the powerful Fujiwara clan. Her obsession with Sukuna is everything to her, and it's clear she would do anything to be with him, even going so far as to propose defeating him in battle if he promises to marry her.

As with Mai, Yorozu's innate technique allows her to create any matter using her cursed energy, as long as she has some knowledge and information about the matter she wishes to create. This lets her make creations that combine the properties of several different elements and materials.

Unfortunately, the cursed energy efficiency of her technique is severely lacking, even though her own cursed energy output is on par with the strongest fighters of the Heian period. This obviously causes great frustration in Yorozu, who dedicated her life to tirelessly researching a way to solve this problem.

Eventually, Yorozu slowly began to glimpse a solution to her technique's weakness. Realizing the energy efficiency of an insect's body, Yorozu came to believe that creating armor incorporating the physical aspects of various insects would largely solve Construction's energy inefficiency. This allows her to fight on the same level as, and even surpass, some of the most powerful jujutsu sorcerers of the Heian period, earning the recognition of the Fujiwara clan.

Using Construction, Yorozu can create more than mere bullets like Mai. One example of this is her insect wings, which allow her to fly at high speeds, or her liquid metal, which she uses as her main combat tool. This liquid metal can change its volume at will, and its stable physical characteristics allow it to take whatever form Yorozu requires for combat by manipulating it with cursed energy.

Yorozu can also create insect armor with the liquid metal. This is the solution to the inefficiency I mentioned earlier; Yorozu considers an insect's shell to be the ultimate armor and the ideal subject for Construction. This greatly enhances her strength, speed, and endurance, improving all her close-quarters combat abilities. It also allows her to increase her skill in mid-range combat by continuing to control liquid metal outside the armor, attacking from multiple fronts.

All of this shows us that Yorozu's control over Construction is exceptionally proficient. Her greatest achievement was even being able to create a perfect sphere, a feat considered impossible. A true sphere has no contact area and generates infinite pressure as a result, making it untouchable. It moves toward Yorozu's target at a moderate speed and becomes an obstacle in combat that her opponent must avoid at all costs or be destroyed, giving them an additional complication in fighting her.

Of course, we can't overlook that Yorozu's techniques don't stop at her mere innate technique; she is skilled enough to have her own domain expansion.

Domain Expansion: Triple Affliction

The setting of this Domain Expansion takes the form of an empty space with several oversized organic structures floating in it, resembling the disembodied brains and ventral nerve cords of insects. The domain's sure-hit effect applies to the objects constructed by the user, and by applying this effect to her true sphere, Yorozu can basically destroy the opponent instantly if they are caught in her domain.

What's interesting about Construction in the context of Jujutsu Kaisen is that, despite seeming like an extremely powerful technique, it's much more difficult to use than it appears, and it requires creativity, effort, and additional study to be as effective as the techniques of the other characters. Both Mai and Yorozu showed us different levels of Construction, and I'm grateful to both of them for giving us one of the best interpretations of this type of technique in any anime or manga series.
