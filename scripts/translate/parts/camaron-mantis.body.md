Not every series makes the most of its secondary characters. Most simply introduce them and then, especially if they're antagonists, discard them without a second thought once they've been defeated. However, one area where Dandadan stands out is in the use and development of its minor antagonists, giving them backstories, motivations, and even future roles that are quite interesting, and there's no better example of this than Peeny Weeny, better known as Mr. Mantis Shrimp.

Surely many of you recognize this muscular fellow with boxing gloves from the Serpo mini-arc as the contractor brought in by the Serpo aliens to finally kidnap Momo and Okarun and steal their banana organs. Yet, despite being an extremely secondary character, the series takes the time not only to show us his combat abilities, but also his motivations and even possible future roles.

The first thing that's obvious from his name is that Mr. Mantis Shrimp is an alien based on... mantis shrimp, a real animal known for the supersonic strike it uses to defend itself from predators and even to hunt its prey. His original form actually doesn't resemble the animal all that much, being a yellow humanoid with a tie, black shorts, and boxing gloves, plus a disc on his head from which his eyes protrude. However, the form modified by the Serpo aliens resembles the animal far more closely, from the particular shape of its supersonic claws to its eyes and coloring.

In terms of his personality, Mr. Mantis Shrimp is a fairly complex individual. When we first see him, he's an aggressive but talkative fighter, who tends to sing or boast about his enhanced strength, or trade words with his opponents. Notably, he's very deferential toward the Serpos, who were his employers, frequently apologizing for his less-than-desirable performance.

Largely as a result of being a weak fighter by his species' standards, Peeny-Weeny, as he's really called, has led a life full of hardship. His wife passed away from a congenital illness, which his son, Chiquitita, also inherited. Because Chiquitita needs regular blood transfusions due to his illness, Mr. Mantis Shrimp takes on dangerous jobs to pay for them. His relative weakness earned him the scorn of his people and the mockery of his employers, which affects him deeply. Therefore, when Momo, Seiko, and company tended to his wounds, provided him with a meal to aid his recovery, and offered a cow for his son's blood transfusions, Mr. Mantis Shrimp was overcome with emotion and vowed to help them in any way he could, showing his gratitude and nobility.

Although he's willing to take part in fights for a just cause, Mr. Mantis Shrimp claims to be a pacifist and refused to teach Okarun how to throw a good punch because he believes violence is wrong. However, he admits that he's prone to lashing out if his patience is tested, such as when he's behind the wheel and his passengers complain about his poor driving skills.

It goes without saying that Mr. Mantis Shrimp's main ability is his incredible physical strength, combined with his boxing technique. He's capable of throwing punches so powerful they can break walls, despite being considered a relatively weak specimen of his species.

His main technique is the Jet Punch, with which he puts all of his strength into a single punch that sends his opponent flying. Although Aira was able to dodge this attack, it's no exaggeration to say it can break bones and do considerable damage if it connects, in addition to being extremely fast.

Mr. Mantis Shrimp is capable of taking on his race's powered-up transformation, called Strong Style 24. In this form, his head resembles that of a mantis shrimp, with bright green eyes. A second pair of large eyes, along with extended antennae, forms the top of his head, which blinks in response to his speech. Around his neck he has a segmented, collar-like structure. His hands transform into the raptorial appendages of a mantis shrimp, with concentric rings of green, red, and orange that can open up and act as shields. His feet are wide and flat, resembling fins or the feet of an aquatic creature.

Ironically, despite the change in appearance that makes him resemble the crustacean, along with a more effective boost in strength in aquatic environments, this form does not grant Mr. Mantis Shrimp gills to breathe underwater. As a result, he must surface occasionally to avoid drowning. He's also limited to remaining in this form for 24 seconds; however, with a special drug, such as a Serpo Energy Drink, his time can be extended to at least 24 hours.

The enhanced physical strength Mr. Mantis Shrimp gains in this form increases his striking power and speed to the point of generating shockwaves with every punch. In addition to allowing him to fight at a distance, these shockwaves prove too difficult for his opponents to dodge, unless they possess extraordinary speed and reflexes.

In keeping with his resemblance to the mantis shrimp, the enhanced strength provided by the form increases significantly when Mr. Mantis Shrimp is submerged underwater. According to him, his strength multiplies tenfold under these conditions.

Also in this form, his Jet Punch changes into an enhanced version when underwater. This version is extremely powerful, capable of destroying high-level armor as well as finishing off both humans and aliens in a single blow.

Lastly, there's the Salaryman Uppercut, a blow that sends his opponent flying upward extremely fast, pinning them to the ceiling, as we can see with poor Aira.

The great thing about Mr. Mantis Shrimp is that he isn't used as an invincible enemy, or even as a wall that teaches the group of protagonists a great deal. His purpose is more to expand the world of the aliens, showing us that there are hierarchies and mundane motivations for the different species, as well as familial love within them, and not just the cold motivations of the Serpo. In fact, his existence makes us wonder what other kinds of aliens might exist in the cosmos, and how they might help or threaten humanity and the Yokai of Earth. Furthermore, Mr. Mantis Shrimp's ability to adapt to a normal job on Earth, and his ability to cooperate with Momo and company, are the first sign of the series introducing aliens as key pieces on humanity's side in its war against being conquered or annihilated, making him, despite being a minor character, someone who actually offers quite important narrative hints in the series.

This great family man with established, strict values and a fairly powerful left hook is actually one of my favorite characters, despite being one who doesn't have much screen time, and I can't wait to see the rest of his exploits adapted into this great anime.
