Cursed spirits in Jujutsu Kaisen are naturally regarded as the source of all evil, being physical representations of human fears. However, throughout the story, the special grade cursed spirits, and especially those in the group of natural disasters, have shown us almost human motivations and goals we can identify with as an audience, such as brotherhood, a desire for justice, and sensitivity toward other beings. Even so, none of them better represents what it means to be an almost neutral entity than Hanami, who at certain moments doesn't even feel like a cursed spirit.

What's interesting about Hanami as a character is that, since she is born from the human fear of the earth's natural disasters, her main motivation, despite seeming evil at first, can be considered by some as something positive. Hanami seeks to end humanity because humanity is slowly destroying nature. In a certain sense, Hanami acts as Mother Nature's force of vengeance, and you could say she is, in a way, the karmic force and the consequence of human actions in causing climate change. In fact, it is mentioned that her cursed energy doesn't feel like that of a cursed spirit, but rather more neutral, like that of a nature spirit.

Hanami is the kind of spirit who cares about the planet that humans have been abusing for thousands of years. She wishes to kill humans to sacrifice them and thus be able to be wise in a cleaner world controlled by cursed spirits who will respect the earth. Hanami believes in Jogo's ideals along with Dagon, since they want to replace humanity with cursed spirits because they feel that curses are the true humans.

In contrast to Hanami's peaceful convictions, Mahito has encouraged them to embrace their savagery as spirits. In the middle of battle, Hanami learned to follow her instincts and began to enjoy the thrill of the fight, which allowed her to push beyond her limits.

Hanami has a close bond with her allies, and in a certain way acts as the mother of the group, even stepping into Gojo's path to save Jogo and looking after everyone behind the scenes. This aspect of her personality has earned her the affection of the other 3 disasters, and even Mahito appreciates her quite a lot.

In terms of power level, Hanami is a special grade cursed spirit with an incredibly durable body, powerful cursed techniques that can control plants, and an unusual ability to hide her presence more effectively than others. She was able to catch Gojo by surprise and momentarily take advantage of Yuji's vulnerability by using her Flower Field and a wood monster to rescue Jogo. This was possible thanks to a combination of Hanami's high intelligence as a curse, as well as her escape abilities and hidden presence. Gojo himself praised Hanami's ability to flee, since they were able to evade the strong perception of the Six Eyes. Satoru even found Hanami much more threatening than the straightforward Jogo.

Hanami possesses an incredibly durable bark around her body, capable of shattering Miwa's sword on impact. She was also able to easily ignore Kamo's Slicing Exorcism attack even though it struck the side of her head. A clean cut to Hanami's leg with Megumi's sword likewise caused no damage. Even after being struck directly in her arm by Maki using the special grade cursed tool: Playful Cloud, she was sent flying but only suffered minimal damage as a result.

After being hit several times by Maki and Megumi in her weak points, Hanami was still able to withstand their attacks, recover quickly, and ultimately overpower them with ease. The most that the majority of the students could hope to achieve was to hold Hanami off long enough for more reinforcements to arrive. Only Yuji and Todo together managed to wound Hanami enough to make her use her full power.

Additionally, due to her nature as a special grade cursed spirit, Hanami can easily reinforce her body with cursed energy to regenerate lost limbs in an instant. The recovered limbs maintain the same integrity as the rest of Hanami's incredibly durable body. She can also regenerate her weak points, the eye branches, but not if they are completely compromised and entirely separated from her body.

Hanami also has a high degree of physical strength and can easily finish off a human sorcerer in hand-to-hand combat. Once Hanami finally closed the distance between her and Kamo, it only took a few blows to incapacitate the sorcerer. But it isn't her close combat that makes her fearsome, but rather her cursed technique.

Innate cursed technique: Disaster Plants

This technique allows the user to form and control a variety of cursed flora. Normally, its abilities are initiated telepathically, activating the technique at the user's side or near a target, but it can also be deployed from the user's body.

The plants created by this technique can vary in size, shape, and physiology. It is possible to form anything from simple flowers to giant wooden roots. However, the technique's flora is still made of cursed energy, which means that curse-like plants can be created quickly and deactivated instantly.

The technique also grants the ability to absorb the life energy of real plants in a floral offering and convert it into cursed energy for the user. This ability can provide a large boost of cursed energy.

In terms of destructive power it may be the weakest of all the natural disasters' techniques; however, its versatility for stealth and its ability to recover cursed energy, combined with Hanami's physical durability, make it a perfect technique to complement the plans of the other three.

In terms of named abilities, Hanami has a wide variety of techniques, although none is especially powerful.

The roots technique creates gigantic roots from the ground and allows her to modify the battlefield terrain, although they are quite slow compared to her other techniques.

The wood ball consists of a dense, compact ball that she uses to throw her opponents and then impale them with thorns.

The cursed sprouts are perhaps her most interesting technique, since they eat the cursed energy of anyone they attach to, growing and eating more energy the more techniques their host uses.

And lastly the flower field, which she uses to distract her opponents so she can connect with her other attacks. This technique is said to be the curse's weakest.

However, we can't forget her domain expansion: the Ceremonial Sea of Light.

Hanami possesses the ability to cast Domain Expansion. The domain's guaranteed-hit ability is the beam that charges with Hanami's left arm. Hanami attempted to expand her domain during her fight against Aoi and Yuji after charging the flower on her shoulder with cursed energy. However, this was interrupted when the curtain keeping Gojo out dissipated. The technique looks fairly simple, allowing Hanami to fire a powerful beam from her shoulder and finish off her opponent in a single blow.

And don't forget Hanami's ability to use Domain Amplification, which is capable of neutralizing all cursed techniques or domains it comes into contact with. This aura or shield covers Hanami's body and even allowed her to eventually strike Gojo through his Limitless, although let's say it didn't go very well for her when she tried to use it in practice.

Despite the fact that, in my opinion, Hanami is the weakest in combat of the 4 natural disasters, her role on the team is very clear and important, being the neutral entity that keeps the others' personalities in check. Additionally, her inspiration and interpretation are the ones I like most, acting more like a karmic judgment than a simple cursed spirit that wants to kill just because. On top of that, although her techniques aren't too effective, their variety and creativity, allowing her to avoid detection even by Gojo's Six Eyes and to steal cursed energy, give her more versatility than those focused solely on destructive power, so we can't ignore this maternal entity of nature.
