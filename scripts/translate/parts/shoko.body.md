The ability to heal with the Reverse Cursed Technique is extremely rare and coveted in the world of Jujutsu Kaisen. The control required to perform it, along with how difficult it is to explain how to achieve it, makes it one of the hardest abilities to master, and only a handful of sorcerers are capable of using it at will. What's interesting is that you don't need to be a genius on Yuta's level to pull it off, and we even have a relatively low-ranked sorcerer who is a specialist in this technique. Of course, I'm talking about Shoko Ieiri, the complement to Geto and Gojo's group at the Tokyo Jujutsu school, and who is currently the academy's healer.

Shoko is a very relaxed and carefree person, especially compared to Ijichi's nervous attitude and Gojo's excessively playful one. As a student alongside Gojo and Geto, she lets the two of them fight and simply removes herself from the situation while calmly climbing the ranks at school. This makes her the perfect complement to these two special grade sorcerers, since she is content to just work behind the scenes and stay out of their way.

Shoko doesn't seem to take any situation very seriously in general during her years at the Jujutsu Academy. She openly smokes in public despite obviously being a high school student, and this habit stayed with her into adulthood. Even after Geto betrayed them, Shoko was able to have a casual conversation with him about what he did. She feels completely comfortable asking him about the accusations and even called him childish for being in a bad mood because no one understands him. She even called Gojo to come confront Geto right in front of him without any fear for her own life.

As the school doctor, Shoko seems to take her job seriously and acts in a very stoic manner. While Gojo was visibly upset over Yuji's death, Shoko kept her personal feelings out of the situation and just wanted to perform an autopsy, pointing out that Gojo was being more emotional than usual. It's hinted that Shoko has tried to quit her smoking habit many times, but the stress of her work as a healer probably makes it harder. She stated that she was "nostalgic for her high school days" when she was noticed smoking again in Shibuya, showing that she still thinks about and cares about what happens to her former classmates and the new students.

As one of the few healers at the Jujutsu Academy and a user of the Reverse Cursed Technique, Shoko is a valuable asset to her colleagues and students alike, and she has saved many sorcerers even on the brink of death. She stays composed on the battlefield and doesn't worry about being discovered in Shibuya despite the danger of being a high-value target.

In terms of power level, it's clear that Shoko is not the combat type of sorcerer, but she still participates in missions with Gojo and Geto while training as a student, which indicates she possesses some kind of combat abilities. Shoko doesn't show the ability to use normal cursed techniques, but she clearly possesses the rare ability to use the Reverse Cursed Technique. She can apply it to others to regenerate lost limbs and heal wounds, something not even Gojo can do. However, when asked how it works, Shoko can't put it into words that could effectively teach someone else how to use it. This shows that she uses it instinctively and doesn't really understand it, which limits its effectiveness.

Shoko possesses the necessary knowledge of a traditional doctor and also has the skills to save her comrades at any given moment. She is invaluable to the sorcerers of the Jujutsu Academy as the only healer of this kind besides Yuta, who is more valuable in combat. When Nanami is injured by Mahito in their first fight, he retreats to the Academy to receive treatment from Shoko. Ijichi and Ino would have died if they weren't treated immediately by her during the Shibuya Incident as well.

Her skills as a doctor also make Shoko very knowledgeable in the field of sorcery in relation to biology. When dissecting two transfigured humans, Shoko is the one who is able to discover that they aren't actually cursed spirits but humans transfigured by a cursed technique. Shoko is also the one who correctly theorizes that the Culling Game's cursed technique removal penalty will affect the players' brains and end their lives as a result.

Even though Shoko doesn't know how to explain it properly, there is a very clear idea of how the Reverse Cursed Technique works.

Basically, this technique processes negative energy into positive energy by taking two sources of cursed energy and multiplying them together. As a result, the energy that destroys becomes energy that creates, and negative energy becomes positive. Despite sounding simple enough, producing positive energy is extremely complex and difficult for most jujutsu users to perform.

Positive energy can be used for various purposes, but most commonly for healing. Cursed energy comes from negative emotions, which are not suitable for healing living beings. However, cursed spirits, which are made up of cursed energy, don't require the Reverse Cursed Technique to heal. Jujutsu sorcerers mainly use positive energy to generate new flesh and heal human bodies. However, even using the Reverse Cursed Technique to heal wounds can sometimes leave a scar. While very few sorcerers can use the Reverse Cursed Technique, there are even fewer who emit it to heal people other than themselves, like Shoko. However, when used to heal others, the Reverse Cursed Technique is less than half as efficient as when healing oneself.

The Reverse Cursed Technique can even be used to replenish a depleted cursed technique after using a Domain Expansion. However, one cannot heal their body at the same time as their cursed technique, and this is something too advanced for Shoko, something we've only seen in the most powerful sorcerers.

The Reverse Cursed Technique can also be used to heal wounds on the soul; however, this requires the user to be able to perceive the shape of the soul, something that Shoko unfortunately is incapable of.

In conclusion, Shoko isn't really the strongest character, not even within her own specialty. However, the special thing is that her ability to use the Reverse Cursed Technique to heal others makes her invaluable to the Jujutsu sorcerers, and someone without whom not only our protagonist but a large part of the cast would have ceased to exist. So we should give this behind-the-scenes support character her due, and maybe understand her and let her smoke a cigarette every now and then to relax.
