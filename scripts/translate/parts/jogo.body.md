Cursed spirits are almost as important as jujutsu sorcerers when it comes to making the world of Jujutsu Kaisen interesting and giving it character as a whole. These innate antagonists have development almost as deep as the protagonists, and some of them show us ambitions, goals, and personalities that even surpass some of the good guys. Among them, Kenjaku's underlings are the ones who have been given the most screen time, and therefore the ones we know best, and none of them is as fiery and passionate as the unregistered special grade volcanic curse. Jogo.

Taking into account the wide spectrum of cursed spirits that have been shown in the Jujutsu Kaisen anime so far, there is no doubt that Jogo leans toward the extreme in practically every aspect. With a fiery temper matched only by the explosive power of his innate cursed technique, Jogo is a character who never walks away from conflict of his own volition, even in situations where he is completely outmatched.

In fact, alongside Mahito, we could say that Jogo is the most recognizable and iconic cursed spirit in Jujutsu Kaisen, and despite having lost the two main fights we saw him take part in, don't think for a second that Jogo is weak. What's more, excluding Sukuna, you could say that Jogo is the strongest cursed spirit we've seen; he's just extremely unlucky to have fought against the two most broken characters in the entire series. If you want proof of his true power against more standard-level characters, ask Nanami, Naobito, or Maki how they measure up against him.

Jogo is defined by his pride as a cursed spirit. He is an intelligent being who considers himself firm in all his beliefs, willing to give his life as long as his goals would be achieved in the long run. On a personal level, Jogo is always grumpy, irritable, and very arrogant.

Jogo believes that cursed spirits are the true humans and that they are the ones who will inherit the earth. He sees humans as creatures made of lies who try to hide their true nature with the positive emotions they display. Curses are composed of negative emotions, which Jogo sees as pure intentions, and so he believes that curses born from that truth are the purest forms of humans and that the false ones should be eliminated. To achieve this, Jogo and his companions agreed to ally themselves with Pseudo-Geto, or Kenjaku, despite him being technically a sorcerer.

Although Jogo is clearly an evil character, his relationship with his fellow cursed spirits is one of camaraderie, friendship, and even family. He sees Dagon and Hanami as his best friends, and his dynamic with Mahito is rivalry but friendly. This contrasts with his attitude when we see him as a villain, when he shows no mercy to humans, being arrogant while crushing them like flies.

In terms of power level, Jogo was one of several unregistered special grades that appeared around 2018. He was born from humanity's fear and negative association with volcanoes and other fire-related natural disasters. This resulted in a highly intelligent cursed spirit with massive levels of cursed energy, even for a special grade. Kenjaku generously placed Jogo at the same strength level as eight or nine of Sukuna's fingers. Although Jogo was no match for overwhelming powers like Gojo and Sukuna, he still far surpassed even the most talented sorcerers and other special grade curses.

Non-sorcerers catch fire in Jogo's very presence if he simply activates his cursed technique. Jujutsu sorcerers who enter his domain would suffer a similar fate. In Shibuya, the Zenin Team and Nanami considered Jogo to be far more powerful than Dagon, whom they had just faced with great difficulty. Jogo defeated Nanami, Maki, and Naobito in mere moments using his superior speed and power. This left half of Nanami's body charred, gave Maki permanent scars, and caused serious injuries to the head of the Zenin clan that soon cost him his life.

Despite not being able to match Sukuna with fifteen fingers absorbed, Jogo's strength was still acknowledged by the King of Curses. Sukuna admitted that Jogo's maximum technique could have hurt him if it had landed. Across all the opponents Sukuna has faced in over a thousand years, Jogo stood out as one of the strongest, a great endorsement of his power overall. Kenjaku even emphasized that he would have liked to absorb Jogo with Cursed Spirit Manipulation and acquire his technique, that's how powerful it was.

Innate cursed technique: Disaster Flames

This technique allows the user to form and control both flames and lava. Its capabilities are not limited to close combat alone, but can also be used telepathically, activating the technique directly on or near a target.

The flames are immensely powerful, capable of instantly killing non-sorcerers and seriously wounding sorcerers. These flames are also visible to non-sorcerers and possess properties similar to real flames. A person with superhuman physical resistance can completely survive these attacks thanks to their body, while a sorcerer's tolerance to curses might not be enough to save their life.

The technique also grants Jogo the ability to telepathically create volcanoes on surfaces. These volcanoes are capable of firing powerful lava at the user's target.

Jogo also has some named techniques that make use of his cursed technique, such as the Ember Insects, with which Jogo conjures small flying insects from his head, whose stingers produce a loud noise upon impact before exploding into flames.

Of course, we can't forget that Jogo is one of the few characters who have shown us the maximization of their technique, showing us the maximum technique, Meteor.

As its name indicates, Maximum: Meteor is a maximum cursed technique that conjures a flaming meteorite that Jogo launches as a projectile. It is relatively small compared to real meteorites, but massive enough to cause widespread devastation on the scale of an entire city. This technique is so powerful that it could even seriously wound Sukuna himself, who was forced to dodge it before finishing off Jogo.

Let's also not forget his domain expansion: Coffin of the Iron Mountain.

The environment built by this domain resembles the interior of an active volcano. It is a sealed stone chamber with large fissures in the walls and floor that expel large portions of magma. This produces such great ambient heat that the average jujutsu sorcerer would catch fire upon entering the domain.

Coffin of the Iron Mountain is a lethal domain that includes a guaranteed hit. The user can command the molten rock extending from the environment to strike his opponent with an attack that cannot miss; of course, if Jogo manages to land this guaranteed hit against almost anyone, his opponent would be instantly burned to a crisp. Unfortunately, when we saw him use the technique, his domain was overwritten by Gojo's, not allowing him to show its true effect.

Last but not least is the Domain Amplification that Jogo is capable of, a technique of manipulating compact domains that allows Jogo to amplify his cursed energy and form an aura capable of neutralizing the effects of a domain expansion's guaranteed hit. Additionally, this technique neutralizes the technique of whatever it touches, providing great flexibility in combat and, in theory, allowing Jogo and the other users to even strike Gojo through his Limitless. Unfortunately, this technique comes with a giant drawback, since it does not allow the user to use their innate technique while it is active, limiting them to hand-to-hand combat.

As you can see, despite his defeats and humiliations throughout the series, Jogo is a strong contender for one of the most powerful cursed spirits ever created, and his flame and lava control abilities are nothing to laugh at. Additionally, his development as a character, his personality, and above all his goals clearly explained to us as the audience make him a character who was incredibly interesting to see expressed in the work, and certainly make him someone relatable to many and even the favorite of some.
