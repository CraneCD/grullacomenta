What is a villain, really? Is it their actions? Their birth? Perhaps their motivations? Is being simply different from humanity a sin that deserves death? Perhaps one of the most interesting characters in Hunter x Hunter is also one of the most dangerous, yet at the same time he is someone who shows more humanity than any of the characters in the series despite not being a human. Meruem is the perfect villain for a series like this one, where morality is a shade of grey, and where even the protagonists who were always good have shown darkness.

Meruem was born a warrior without a heart, respecting people only for their ability to withstand combat with him, but he would eventually become a kind and compassionate pacifist, valuing the time spent with those he cherished even above his own pride. Throughout the Chimera Ant arc, Meruem fought and constantly showed signs of humanity in contrast to his biological birth as a Chimera Ant.

Meruem was born a cruel and violent leader. The Queen's intention to give birth to a perfect offspring was reflected in Meruem's mentality. Inside the womb, he ignored his mother's concern for his development and tore through her abdomen, forcing his own birth. He felt no concern for his dying mother or for the other ants, killing and cannibalizing several Chimera Ants that had decided to tend to the Queen's wounds rather than attend to him. Neferpitou would be the first person to withstand an attack from Meruem, after enduring an attack that Meruem tried to kill her with without defending herself. He showed no empathy toward humans and saw them as nothing more than a source of food, killing and devouring a child shortly after his birth.

Meruem would only begin to change upon meeting perhaps the weakest human of all, Komugi, which later evolved into a deep affection for her. With this change of heart, Meruem began to question the differences between the Ants and humanity, and he struggled with his identity as half human and half insect, questioning the nature of violence and strength as true power. After his interactions with Komugi and his fight against Netero, Meruem began to see humans as incredible beings with immense potential through their tenacity to surpass their own limits. He showed great respect and complete admiration for Netero, seeing his abilities and fighting style as truly magnificent, and he constantly praised him during their battle, referring to him as a rare example of a human who has transcended his limitations and reached the pinnacle of human capabilities. Because of this change of perspective, Meruem no longer considered farming humans for food and instead suggested the coexistence of humans and Ants, with humans being selected and controlled by their quality.

The traits that define Meruem are his pride and his self-confidence. From his birth to his death, Meruem displayed a strong confidence in himself and conviction in his actions. He insisted on proving his intellectual capabilities to himself by defeating the champions of various board games at their own games shortly after learning their rules. However, after losing several games against Komugi, he showed his first sign of human emotion, trying to scare and distract her during a game of Gungi by threatening to tear off her arm if she lost. Komugi managed to exceed his expectations and responded with such respect toward him and the game that Meruem tore off his own arm in apology for his insolence and disrespect toward her and the game.

He considered certain concepts of human society to be absurd. Examples of this were when Shaiapouf explained how certain people could become rulers through connections or lineage rather than power, something Meruem saw as a contradiction to all natural logic. When he understood the injustice of human society, where children starve to death while the pathetic weak have everything they need, he wanted to destroy this madness and create a new world where the concept of inequality would be completely forgotten. Furthermore, he came to believe that the true purpose of power is to protect the weak who deserve to live, not to torment the defeated.

He was highly skilled and learned things at an astonishing rate, being able to master any form of board game in only ten matches and defeat world champions with ease. After a brief loss of memory, he seemed to become much more respectful and close to his subordinates after having been fed a part of them when he was dying, as he could feel what they felt and easily tell whether or not they were hiding something from him; at the same time, he could perceive their loyalty toward him, which led him to test their loyalty before considering punishing them, all the more so if they answered with the truth despite doing so meaning admitting a miscalculation. The sense of loyalty he felt from them was so strong that it prevented Meruem from killing both Pouf and Youpi for lying to him, despite having previously told them not to lie to him.

Despite his extreme arrogance and superiority complex, he felt fear for the first time when Netero prepared to detonate the Poor Man's Rose and unleashed an incredibly malevolent aura and supreme confidence despite being on the brink of death. Moments before succumbing to the poison, he admitted to Palm Siberia that humanity had won and was willing to accept his defeat. His desire to fulfill his last wish was so strong that he was willing to kneel before her.

In terms of power level, despite being a newborn, Meruem is the most powerful character introduced in the series so far, as well as the one possessing the greatest learning capacity. Born as the ultimate biological weapon, his tremendous physical prowess is matched only by an incomparable intellect, both of which seem to place him far beyond the realm of human possibility. As the King of the Chimera Ants, his power surpasses even that of the Royal Guards, and he can increase it further by consuming Nen users. He fought Isaac Netero, who was previously considered the strongest Nen user in the world and who maintained a formidable power level even in his old age, while trying not to kill him, a condition Meruem compared to playing chess without a rook or a bishop, and he won their physical confrontation. After absorbing most of the bodies of Shaiapouf and Menthuthuyoupi, Knuckle believed that state-level military power would be necessary to match him. As king of a Chimera Ant colony, Meruem has authority over the Royal Guards and the ability to procreate with females of different species.

As soon as he was born, Meruem already possessed enough strength to blow off the heads of several Squadron Leaders with a single strike of his tail in each case, as well as to pierce a hole in the nest with such force that the entire structure shook. By driving his tail into the outer wall of the nest, he was able to launch himself high into the air. His tail is so powerful that it allowed him to wound all three Royal Guards, though only slightly, although he failed to kill Neferpitou and held back on at least one other occasion. He was able to effortlessly cut off Netero's left arm and right leg, as well as, even more remarkably, his own left arm, despite the outstanding durability of his body. With the help of Ren, he managed to push back two of the hands of Netero's 100-Type Guanyin Bodhisattva.

Meruem could move so quickly that, a few moments after his birth, he decapitated two Squadron Leaders without them or Colt managing to see him move. The Royal Guards also did not seem able to react. In the brief instant when Netero and Zeno were distracted by Neferpitou's aura, Meruem managed to calmly walk between them without being detected. By his own admission, the only time Netero's speed surpassed his was during the movements of Netero's hands when activating the 100-Type Guanyin Bodhisattva. In the final part of their duel, with both fighters' senses at their peak, they exchanged more than a thousand blows in less than a minute: at the very least, this means that Meruem regained his balance after being pushed by Netero and launched a new offensive more than 16 times per second. However, Netero's hand movements were so fast that Meruem could not see his first attack, and only thanks to the psychological phenomenon known as Spirit Echoes did he barely manage to keep pace, until he finally managed to dodge a flawed counterattack. After absorbing Menthuthuyoupi and Shaiapouf, he became so fast that he reached and knocked out Meleoron and Knuckle immediately after their En registered him, giving them the impression that he had teleported, and he knocked them out without either of them being able to react. He also acquired the ability to fly, which increased his already supersonic speed to the point that Shaiapouf believed it had quadrupled.

Thanks to his astonishing memory and analytical skills, Meruem mastered multiple board games just by reading the rulebooks and defeated three national champions in only a few matches, a time that decreased as he learned more games. He is able to examine multiple scenarios in a matter of seconds. He excels at identifying his opponent's "rhythm," the unconscious bias that guides their choices, and at disrupting it or using it against them, not only gaining information about their thought process but also managing to exploit it in his favor. Playing Gungi with Komugi elevated his pattern-recognition skills to an ability akin to precognition. Thanks to this faculty, he was able to penetrate Netero's otherwise impenetrable defense, even predicting in advance which parts of the body he could cut.

As far as his Nen is concerned, Meruem is an Emitter. He was born with the ability to use Nen and with an enormously superhuman amount of aura, which surpassed even that of the Royal Guards. By feeding on Nen users, he further increased the size of his aura. After consuming Shaiapouf and Menthuthuyoupi, his aura reserves and production became so vast that even the overprotective Royal Guards had to admit that the King seemed invincible and capable of winning a Nen battle through the sheer quantity of his aura alone. Upon sensing his Ren, the members of the Extermination Team despaired, with Knuckle believing that they would need the military power of an entire country to defeat the King.

He was able to perform Gyo shortly after his birth without receiving any instruction. His Ren instilled fear even into Netero's heart, and it allowed Meruem to open the hands of the 100-Type Guanyin Bodhisattva, as well as to emerge completely unharmed from the Third Arm, whereas Netero's First Arm had previously made him spit blood. This defensive application of Ren may imply that he is capable of using Ken.

The first time he used En, it covered an area far larger than the palace grounds and expanded at the speed of light. Despite its vastness, his control over it is so refined that he was able to discern changes in the environment as small as fresh footprints. At first, without his knowing it, his En transmuted part of his aura into photons that transmitted information to him. When, after only two uses, he realized this characteristic of his En, he was able to turn it into a Nen ability. The absorption of Shaiapouf and Menthuthuyoupi granted him variations of their Nen abilities, which he was able to activate successfully on the first attempt.

These are some of his Nen abilities.

Aura Synthesis

Meruem's initial ability grants him strength through consumption. His aura grows each time he devours a Nen user, as their aura is synthesized with Meruem's. The amount of the body he needs to consume to achieve this is unknown, but it seems that Meruem must absorb most of their body in order to use his ability. Neferpitou suggested that this is a Nen ability rather than (merely) a biological one.

Metamorphosis

After absorbing large parts of Shaiapouf and Menthuthuyoupi, Meruem is able to use Menthuthuyoupi's ability to transform, including the ability to generate wings to fly. This allows him to fly at such high speed that he was able to cover a distance that Menthuthuyoupi and Shaiapouf estimated would take 15 minutes in only 5 minutes.

Outburst of Rage

After absorbing large parts of Shaiapouf and Menthuthuyoupi, Meruem acquired the ability to fire explosive bursts of aura from his arm. A single burst has enough power to severely damage a large rock formation.

Photon:

By combining the abilities inherited from Shaiapouf with his own En, Meruem created his own version of Spiritual Message. It consists of converting his aura into microscopic photon-like particles and deploying them with his En in an instant, or of detaching the photons from his body and scattering them into the surrounding air. Each photon retains its aura even when separated from Meruem's body. This ability allows him to measure the shape, quality, emotion, and other informational content of the things the photons interact with; therefore, this ability likely also involves Emission and Manipulation. According to Welfin and Shaiapouf, Meruem can detect everything, even lies. The accuracy of the information he can gather depends on the number of photons directed at the object of interest. It is also briefly shown that, immediately after deploying the photons in a flash, Meruem appears to teleport to the immediate vicinity of any target of interest within the affected region. However, it is unclear whether this is actually part of this ability or whether Meruem is simply covering extraordinary distances instantly with his immense speed.

It is clear that the interesting part of Meruem's character is his evolution into becoming human and his contrast with the so-called humans of the arc. This, and his relationship with Komugi, overshadow the specific abilities of the king of the ants, and that is why almost no one is able to tell you what his specific abilities are, with the exception that he is the strongest of all. Characters like Meruem are seen very rarely in anime, and I would dare to say that his execution is what makes this arc as valued as it is.
