In the real world as much as in anime and manga, everything is about balance. Sometimes, when you have impulsive and relentless characters, you also need a shy and cautious one in order to show the audience both sides of the coin and make the story feel more real. Likewise, these shy characters who think before they act generally offer the best opportunities for development, allowing them, through effort and motivation, to break out of those birdcages in which they encase themselves and to reach new heights. In Hunter X Hunter the best example of this is Shoot, who, despite being overshadowed by the rest of the characters in the Chimera Ant arc, is probably one of the most developed characters, and has one of the most interesting abilities of the arc.

Shoot has a shy personality and is often afraid to seize the opportune moments to attack an enemy. His prudence makes him a good contrast to Knuckle, since he counters the latter's impulsiveness with his colder and more analytical mind. He was willing to let thousands of people die for the sake of saving billions of others, showing himself to be someone Machiavellian. However, when the fight with Youpi begins, he is inspired by Gon and is able to unleash his full power, overcoming his shyness and his wounds.

We tend to think of Hunters as people who love to fight in order to achieve their goals; however, Shoot is a great example of the opposite. He is someone who, by his own confession, does not like to hurt others, not even if they deserve it, and fears for his own well-being more than most of his companions. This can make him feel like someone hesitant, and maybe he is, but it is his ability to overcome this and give everything of himself to protect his companions despite not having this tendency naturally that makes him so inspiring.

In terms of power level, Shoot has demonstrated remarkable combat prowess by briefly facing Youpi, a Royal Guard, on his own, although he suffered nearly fatal wounds. Additionally, with Knuckle's help, he was able to seal the manipulated body of Kite, who was fighting without restrictions of any kind, an extremely notable achievement. His low confidence in his abilities has often been an obstacle for him, but Gon's example allowed him to discover his own determination, which enabled him to attack Youpi without worrying about his own life. He is very observant, noticing Killua's electric attack before he could carry it out. His Nen abilities allow him to effectively compensate for the loss of his left arm.

Perhaps Shoot's most important physical attribute is his speed, since he can move so quickly that the slightest distraction from Killua allowed him to close the distance between them before the young Hunter could react, although he managed to avoid the subsequent blows when he was not under the effects of his brother's conditioning. Shoot noticed electricity around Killua's hands and withdrew before Killua's ability could connect. While flying on his hand, he becomes extremely fast, dodging and redirecting many of Youpi's attacks despite their number, and even managed to touch the Royal Guard's body once.

Some of Shoot's most overlooked abilities are his spatial reasoning and balance, both key to being able to use his ability at the level he uses it, and despite being someone who does not like to fight and fears getting hurt, his stamina and resistance to pain are remarkable, being able to keep fighting after Youpi's attacks to his leg and after suffering lethal wounds all over his body.

Finally, Shoot is one of the most expert Hunters in pursuit and hunting, being able to follow his prey's tracks expertly and better than most other Hunters.

When it comes to his Nen, Shoot is a Manipulator*. He specializes in manipulating three hands that he uses as controllable projectiles or as distractions to land a blow and activate Hotel Rafflesia. The repeated disappearances and reappearances of his birdcage, and especially of the hands, seem to suggest that they are conjured. Since he does not like to hurt others, Shoot made his main Nen ability one that can only be used by injuring someone, so he would resort to it when he had no other option and, therefore, would have no qualms about using it. Another of his abilities allows him to convert small objects into two-dimensional impressions in his right hand. He is an extremely capable user of Zetsu who went unnoticed by Gon and Killua for nearly a month. He also appears to be competent in Shu, since he is able to imbue his floating hands with aura. During the attack on the Chimera Ants, following a hunch, Shoot covered his right eye, which, together with the thrill of feeling cornered, increased his strength, indicating that he is potentially a user of Nen restrictions.

Going into more detail on his Hatsu, which can be a little confusing to understand, Shoot calls it Impression Conversion, which uses his Nen to convert pocket-sized three-dimensional objects into impressions on the palm of his hand, as well as returning them to their normal state without damage. It seems that Shoot can maintain the effect for at least several days. This allows him to use two sub-techniques, the first of these, and the one we most often see him use, being the floating hands.

Shoot can conjure three floating hands, which he then controls with Manipulation. He can coat them with aura and use them as projectiles capable of surrounding and striking the target, but also as distractions to get close to the opponent and activate Hotel Rafflesia. Shoot has great control over the hands, which he can position to exploit the blind spots created by Hotel Rafflesia. The aura around a single hand allows him to have enough power to damage Killua, though not enough to hurt him significantly, as well as to come out unharmed from Killua's attacks, both physical and through his yo-yos. During the fight against Youpi, Shoot was able to control the hands with such precision, speed and power that they were able to defend against the Royal Guard's tentacles. He could also stand on one of them to fly quickly, which he considers his special technique. Despite the fact that Shoot always uses the hands together with the cage of Hotel Rafflesia, the two abilities seem distinct.

But now that I mention it, what is Hotel Rafflesia?

This second ability allows him, through physical contact, to steal specific parts of the opponent's body through physical contact, shrinking them and teleporting them to his birdcage, potentially until their entire body is sealed. The affected body part is replaced by a dark mist. Although the opponent feels no pain and is not actually injured, Shoot's ability can throw their mind into chaos. Furthermore, by targeting the eyes, Shoot can create blind spots to exploit in successive attacks. To deactivate the ability, Shoot opens the door of the cage, allowing the trapped target to walk out, after which they return to their normal size. It deactivates automatically if Shoot loses consciousness. Given that the size reduction is associated with Conjuration, it seems that Hotel Rafflesia also incorporates elements of this type of Nen. Its teleportation component may mean the additional involvement of Emission.

Shoot's abilities are interesting because, in addition to being useful in situations that are not only combat but also support, they are abilities that contrast greatly with his personality, which is completely intentional. You see, Shoot is one of the few characters in Hunter x Hunter who knows his flaws and limitations perfectly, and he uses his technique as a way to force himself to set aside his shyness and to hurt others. This technique, which requires stealing others' bodies from them and striking them from multiple directions, is as much a torture for his opponents as for himself, and only after Gon's help and inspiration did he finally manage to reach his potential.

Sometimes you hear criticism of Shoot for his indecision, shyness and cowardice. But these critics do not stop to think about the effects that these qualities have in an arc where most of the characters are overly impulsive and decisive, and who, if they would only stop to think for a second, could obtain a better result. This, added to his rapid development and the lesson his character teaches the audience, makes him someone worthy of participating in this incredible arc and of being regarded as an essential character for this wonderful Shonen.
