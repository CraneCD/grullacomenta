Motivations are inseparable parts of characters. Those aspects of their past, or of their desires for the future, that move them and shape their personality are what make them who they are, and there is no more important motivator in the world of Hunter x Hunter than revenge. Revenge for your loved ones, revenge for yourself, or revenge for your clan. This powerful feeling is enough to bring out a person's potential, and in Kurapika's case, it is his entire being, what dictates his every move and even what limits and empowers his hatsu and all of his Nen abilities.

Kurapika is my favorite character in Hunter x Hunter, and his Hatsu is my favorite Nen ability in the entire series, and this is due to its complexity but also its focus toward a single objective. Kurapika is the last survivor of the Kurta clan, or the clan of the scarlet eyes, after his entire clan was hunted down for their valuable eyes. This naturally explains his character's obsession with recovering the precious relics of his fellow clansmen, and it is all he can think about most of the time.

However, this does not mean Kurapika is one-dimensional; if anything, I would dare say that the internal conflict he shows us, and his attitudes toward the different kinds of people he encounters throughout the arcs, make him one of the most complex and profound.

We first meet Kurapika in the Hunter Exam arc as an intelligent, kind, and honest boy who makes friends easily and has an incredible latent talent as a Hunter. However, even in this arc, the darkness within him suddenly begins to show whenever the subject of his eyes or his clan is touched upon, and it reveals his uncharacteristic loss of self-control when it comes to this part of his being.

Of course, in York New, Kurapika could be considered the protagonist, and it is here that we learn the extent of what this boy is capable of doing and sacrificing in order to achieve his revenge. This dark arc perfectly reflects the darkness and loneliness in Kurapika's soul, and just as the Hunter Exam arc showed us his light with touches of darkness, this arc shows the black void in his heart, but not without showing us, in his interactions with Melody and his companions, that there is light in there too. These small displays of kindness show us the other side of Kurapika that we surely forget beneath his hatred and bloodlust, and they make us wonder which side is the true one.

From this point on Kurapika becomes more mysterious; we are mostly absent from him for the rest of the arcs. However, after passing the endpoint of the anime adaptation and venturing into the manga-exclusive arcs, we return to Kurapika as protagonist, and we come to understand not only his dark and light sides, but his intelligence, his tactical skills, and the sheer versatility of his ability and Nen.

In terms of power level, Kurapika's powers increase enormously as the series progresses. Hisoka was the first to take an interest in him, classifying him as one of the "green fruits" with vast potential. Although he was weaker than Killua at first, by the Yorknew arc he had become stronger than him thanks to his Nen abilities. He is one of the few characters to have killed a member of the infamous Phantom Troupe, and a very powerful member at that, in Uvogin. His exceptional intelligence is on par with his great combat prowess and his iron will, which stems from his desire for revenge. He is also a very versatile fighter, as he has been seen wielding different weapons. In his confrontation with Uvogin, he used his chains both at range and in close-quarters combat, and he has been seen using a Bokken, knives, Shuriken, and even firearms such as pistols.

Of course, if we are going to talk about his power level, we cannot leave aside his Scarlet Eyes. When a member of the Kurta Clan becomes enraged, their eyes turn a vivid shade of red, which triggers an astonishing increase in physical strength. In this state, even as a child, Kurapika could gain the advantage in terms of speed and strength against three adults and overpower them easily. However, Kurapika admitted that when his eyes turn scarlet, he loses all restraint and rationality and becomes primal, which also happens whenever he sees a Spider. Furthermore, excessive use of his Scarlet Eyes can have serious repercussions on his health, as doing so left him incapacitated and feverish for more than a day, with Melody's Nen ability unable to bring his body temperature down. His current limit is about 3 hours, after which the pain and fatigue become too intense and he passes out for three times as long. Kurapika also speculates that prolonged repeated use, or uses induced by stealing particularly powerful abilities, may cause him to reach the limit before 3 hours have passed. At some point during his Nen training, Kurapika learned to turn his eyes scarlet at will and gained the ability to control his emotions.

Kurapika is one of the most brilliant characters in the series. Because of this, he was named leader of Neon's bodyguards after Dalzollene's death. He outmaneuvered minds on the level of Chrollo with carefully planned strategies, and his predictions have rarely turned out to be wrong. He also has immense knowledge on many subjects, ranging from psychology to other cultures, from history to criminology, a quality that surprised his companions when he was just a child. Due to his thirst for new knowledge, he learns quickly and has an impressive memory. Kurapika's analytical skills are so outstanding that they have led to a rumor spreading among the assassins of Muherr that he is capable of reading minds. While searching for Halkenburg in Kakin's bodyguard registry, he used the vaguest of clues to pick out the person he felt was Halkenburg, something Oito praised.

Kurapika can formulate flawless plans even in combat to fully exploit his opponent's weaknesses. His intuition is combined with his logical thinking and meticulous preparations. Although he can stay calm in desperate situations, he can barely stick to his own plan when it comes to the Phantom Troupe, to the point where he might even lose control and endanger not only himself but also his friends. On the Black Whale, he showed greater composure than before and pulled off his greatest strategic feat to date, becoming the linchpin of a stalemate, or at least an obstacle, in the succession contest with an endless series of tactics, mixing truth and lies, secrets and deliberately disclosed information. His disclosure of the existence of Nen and its categories constituted the biggest shift in the succession contest, forcing an impasse and increasing the survival odds of the lower-ranked princes.

As far as his Nen goes, Kurapika is a Conjurer, a Nen user who can create objects out of nothing according to his imagination, but he becomes a Specialist when his eyes turn scarlet. He learned Nen in very little time and is considered a highly skilled user. In terms of theoretical knowledge, he can divide Nen learners by aptitude, and he is an expert in the mechanics of Nen Limitations and Manipulation. Kurapika has Nen abilities that incorporate elements of Emission, Manipulation, and Enhancement; in addition, he has shown proficiency in Transmutation, proving to be a complete Nen user and one of the few who can access all six categories of Nen. The weapon he conjures is unique: five chains that extend from each finger of his right hand. Some of the fingers were specially designed to take revenge on the Phantom Troupe. To increase the effectiveness of his chains against them, Kurapika imposed strict Limitations on the use of his chains. Since the chains are materialized, their size and length can be controlled to a certain extent. He is able to hide his chain through In to bind his opponent without them realizing it until it is too late. Kurapika is also proficient in other branches of Nen, such as Shu, which, combined with Emperor Time, gives his chains the ability to break rocks. He has reached such a high level of skill that he can remain in a state of Ten without conscious thought.

By using a Limitation that puts his own life at risk, his abilities received a great boost. In fact, one of his chains is so strong that Uvogin, an experienced fighter, assumed it was a real chain that Kurapika was manipulating. The combination of his Limitation, the increase in ability granted by his Specialization power, and the aura boost granted by his Scarlet Eyes allowed him to close the gap in strength against a master Enhancer like Uvogin. Repeated use of his Scarlet Eyes combined with Nen takes a great toll on his mind and body, causing a high and incapacitating fever that, after Pakunoda's death, lasted several days and could not be relieved by Melody's flute.

Let's talk about each of Kurapika's chains.

Dowsing Chain:

A chain that ends with a small ball-shaped weight, which Kurapika frequently uses in combat for both offensive and defensive purposes. Its main use is as a tool for pendulum dowsing, with the ball moving according to Kurapika's objective at any given moment. As such, it is useful for investigation, since the chain can be used to determine the location of missing persons (moving in their direction) and to identify whether someone is lying (moving back and forth). If the chain's ability is used on a group, it works best if the people being questioned answer one at a time. Under normal conditions, Kurapika must be directly in front of the target for lie detection to be effective; however, when Emperor Time is active, he can also use that ability through video recordings, as long as he has met the subject before. Kurapika himself does not know how this aspect of his power works; his guess is that, through the transmission of the chain and extreme concentration, he can unconsciously detect subtle signs that someone is lying.

Chain Jail

An extremely durable chain with a hook on the end, whose sole purpose is to capture members of the Phantom Troupe and deprive them of their freedom. It forces captives into a state of Zetsu, shutting off their aura and preventing the use of Nen abilities; consequently, the only way to escape this chain is through brute force, although not even Uvogin, the physically strongest member of the Spiders, could break free from it. The chain manifests so quickly that Kurapika can completely immobilize a distant target in half a second. To create the jail, Kurapika imposed the Limitation that it can only be used on members of the Spiders, with the additional stipulation that violating this rule will result in his death.

Emperor Time

When Kurapika's eyes turn scarlet, he changes from a Conjurer to a Specialist. This ability allows him to use all types of Nen at 100% efficiency (for a Conjurer). Kurapika explains this using the terms "Level," "Strength," and "Precision": his "Levels" in the various Nen categories remain the same during Emperor Time, but his "Strength" and "Precision" for each category are raised to 100%. Thus, if Kurapika's "Level" in Conjuration was 10, his "Level" in Emission (the polar opposite aura type) would still be 4 at most, but his "Strength" and "Precision" in any Emission ability would increase from 40% to 100%, making him an even match against a Level 4 Emitter. However, the price to pay is considerable, since every second spent in this mode reduces Kurapika's lifespan by one hour.

Holy Chain

Through his cross pendant, this chain heals wounds by drawing on Enhancement-type Nen. When Kurapika is in his Specialist state, the chain can heal serious injuries, such as a fractured arm, in a matter of seconds. Apart from this it does not seem to have many other uses, but having a healing ability is extremely useful for Kurapika given the level of danger of his main mission.

Judgment Chain

Through the blade at its end, called the stake of vengeance, this chain enters a victim's body and coils around their heart. Kurapika then declares a binding rule that, if violated by the victim, causes the stake to pierce their heart and kill them. Kurapika himself has a blade around his own heart, which he used to set the limitations on the use of the Chain Jail ability. He is able to issue at least two commands per stake, and he can dissolve the chain at will, and he is certain that he would be alerted if a member of the Spiders removed it. Due to Kurapika's low affinity and mastery in Emission and Manipulation, the Judgment Chain can only be used during Emperor Time.

Stealing Chain

With a syringe-shaped end, this chain, upon piercing a target's body, extracts their aura and steals one of their Nen abilities. While their aura is being drained (a process that seems painful), the target is forced into a Zetsu-like state. Afterward, the target cannot use the stolen ability until it is returned to them. There are no conditions for stealing an ability other than piercing the target with the syringe: so far, Kurapika has stolen two abilities, one after the owner's Nen type was revealed and the general mechanics of the ability explained, and another that was about to be used without any prior knowledge. The death of the original owner does not result in Kurapika losing possession of a stolen ability.

Stealth Dolphin

By activating Emperor Time, Kurapika can summon a dolphin-like Nen beast with a syringe on each pectoral fin that is visible and audible only to him. This construct can "equip" and analyze the stolen ability, informing Kurapika of its name, functions, and conditions. Kurapika can then use the stolen ability himself or lend it to another person, including non-users, by jabbing them with the Stealing Chain. In the latter case, a listening device is conjured in that person's ear, allowing them to see the dolphin and communicate with it through telepathy. The dolphin can advise the new recipient on how to use the ability, and it will use the ability itself when ordered to by the recipient of the lent ability. When Kurapika, or someone he has lent an ability to, activates it, it is fueled by the aura that the Stealing Chain extracted from its owner, ensuring it is used with the original power.

A side effect of the Stealth Dolphin is that when it is used on a non-user, it allows them to see aura and, after the lent ability has been activated, it half-opens all of their aura nodes, essentially making them Nen users, although training is still required to control the flow of aura and prevent it from leaking.

If Kurapika equips the dolphin with a stolen ability and transfers it to another person, he can use the Stealing Chain again, but the transfer does not allow him to leave Emperor Time until he calls the dolphin back, and the stolen ability must be used in order to do so. Regardless of who controls the stolen ability, it can only be used once, after which it returns to the original user. As a drawback, as long as all stolen abilities have not been returned, Emperor Time will remain active. The dolphin will regularly notify Kurapika of how long it has been active unless it is put into standby mode, in which case it can be made to disappear. If Kurapika passes out while another person is using an ability he has lent them, that person will pass out as well.

Of course, we cannot talk about the Stealing Chain and the Stealth Dolphin without talking about Kurapika's stolen abilities, so let's go through them quickly:

Little Eye:

Kurapika stole this ability from Sayird. With it, the user throws a ball of aura at an insect or any other small, living creature, capturing it and gaining control over it. The largest creature the user can control is about the size of a hamster. The user gains knowledge of everything the manipulated animal sees or hears. For this reason, the ability is particularly useful when used on flies or mosquitoes, but since they have many natural predators, they tend to die while under the effect of the ability. Faster animals can be difficult to catch. The ability cannot be used on living beings created through aura. The controlled animal retains the user's ability to see aura. This ability seems to consume very little aura, since even a person who was not originally a Nen user could maintain it for an hour without noticeable strain. Moreover, it does not deactivate if the user passes out.

Air Blow

Kurapika stole this ability from Vincent. Little is known about it. The original user tried to activate it through his left palm to break the guard of a Nen user who was capable of stopping bullets with his aura. It seems it does not require direct contact with the target to work, and we can infer that it is a blow of air that can stun or open up an enemy's guard.

Erigeron

Lastly, Bill voluntarily allowed Kurapika to steal this ability, which dramatically accelerates the growth of living beings when the user brings their palms close to them. Its main use is to develop other people's Nen abilities, although the effects on people with little talent are quite mild.

As you can see, Kurapika's Nen abilities, like his character, are extensive and complex, which sometimes makes them a little difficult to understand. However, the objective of both is clear: to wipe out the Spiders and recover the scarlet eyes of his fellow clansmen, and although he takes it a bit to the extreme, I think we can all identify with Kurapika a little, whether in his darkest moments or his most illuminated ones.
