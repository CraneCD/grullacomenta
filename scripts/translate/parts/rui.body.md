One of the main themes in Kimetsu no Yaiba is the bond of family. This is highlighted primarily by showing the bond between Tanjiro and his sister Nezuko. His determination and perseverance show just how far he is willing to go to protect a loved one.

However, the series also shows that it is not only humans who desire a family bond, but demons as well. The Spider Family, who lived on Mount Natagumo, was one of the demonic families shown in the series, a family that was created by a lower-ranked Kizuki, Rui, our topic for today.

Rui was once a human boy who was born weak and frail, and spent most of his life trapped in his home, cared for by his parents. Rui was desperate to be a normal child, and he got the chance when he met Muzan, who offered to give him strength and power like no other. Rui was then turned into a demon with spider-like demonic abilities, but like all demons, he needed to eat humans to survive, and he ended up killing people. Once, when his parents discovered the body of a dead man, they grew afraid and believed that killing Rui was the way to stop all the murders, but before his father could attack him with a knife, Rui woke up and ended up killing them both. Shocked by the fact that his own parents were willing to kill him.

As his parents lay dying, Rui heard the final words spoken by his mother, saying that she was sorry she could not give him a better life and a healthy body. Rui then remembered the last words his father said as he raised the knife to kill him, that he should not worry because they were going to kill themselves right after, so that the three of them could live together peacefully in the afterlife. Rui then understood that this was an act of love and not of ill intent, but the realization came too late. Traumatized by his actions, Rui deeply longed for a family once again. As the years passed, Rui became a powerful demon, so powerful that Muzan gave him a large dose of his blood so that Rui could grow even stronger.

Muzan favored Rui so much that he gave him permission to create a family of his own to fill the void of his absent family. Thus began the beginnings of the Spider Family. Rui began to seek out demons, offering them protection from the Demon Slayers, but in exchange, they had to join his family. If they accepted, Rui would kill the Demon Slayers pursuing them and then take the demon to his home, where he would start a welcoming ceremony by making them drink a drop of Muzan's blood, which made them stronger. Then, Rui would transform them so that they had appearances similar to his own and assign roles such as father, mother, brother, or sister. In this way, Rui positioned himself as the younger brother even though he was clearly the strongest and the leader of the family.

The interesting thing about this tendency of Rui's is that it made him weaker. The fact that he shared Muzan's blood meant that he was not as powerful as he should have been, and this limited him to being a mere lower moon. It is possible that if Rui had consumed the blood by himself, and had set aside his desire to have a family, he would have managed to become an upper moon.

Of course, Rui does not only have his family as part of his combat power, but he also possesses powerful techniques based on:

His Blood Demon Art: Thread Manipulation

Rui's Blood Demon Art allows him to create thin threads indistinguishable from silk from his own flesh and manipulate them at will. Normally, he extends the threads from his fingers like a puppeteer. Due to the durability of his own body, the threads Rui manipulates are extremely tough and sharp, capable of cutting as well as Nichirin Swords and blocking attacks from the latter as well. Rui can further increase their strength and durability by infusing his blood through them, giving them a blood-red color.

Rui displayed terrifying skill in using his threads for combat purposes, making use of the environment to launch an avalanche of cutting attacks or to manifest inescapable web techniques to trap and cut his opponents to pieces. These are the named techniques that Rui showed us:

Cutting Thread Cage: As its name indicates, with this technique Rui creates a kind of cage like a spider web, which he uses to surround his opponent and eventually cut them from all sides. Although powerful in the sense that it is hard to escape from, it is not Rui's fastest technique, and the threads are not the toughest, so an opponent can quickly dodge or cut the threads to escape.

Murderous Eye Basket: Similar to the previous technique, this technique consists of surrounding his opponent with threads to then shrink them and cut the opponent, the difference seeming to lie in the way it is used, doing a childish cat's cradle game with the hands, and it is used against a previously incapacitated opponent.

Cutting Thread Rotation: This is Rui's most powerful technique, with which he uses control of his threads to make an intense rotation of them and concentrate them into a single wave of attack that covers a fairly extensive area. Unlike his other techniques, the speed of this one, mixed with the concentration of Rui's flesh in the threads, makes it extremely powerful, and it could surely finish off any slayer who was struck head-on by it.

Despite Rui not being an upper moon, and appearing in only part of the first season of Kimetsu, his themes are more interesting than even some of the upper Kizuki, and he is the first real enemy we see in the series, giving him great value for Tanjiro's growth. Additionally, Rui is presented as a powerful character who served to demonstrate the true power of the Hashira, especially Giyu, serving a role pointing toward the future of where our protagonist could one day reach, and for this we should give this little white spider the respect it deserves.
