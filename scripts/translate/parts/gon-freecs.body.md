As many of you surely know, power systems are especially interesting to me, and surely to many of you as well. Shonen tend to have innovative ability systems that deepen their characters, and they vary quite a bit in their levels of complexity. However, there is one that stands out from the rest, and that is even used as the foundation for one of the most praised shonen of the moment. Of course, I'm talking about the Nen of the acclaimed Hunter x Hunter. And what better way to begin talking about this incredibly interesting and complex power system than through the protagonist of its series, Gon Freecs.

Gon is an extremely interesting protagonist, because although he comes across as fairly typical in the early seasons, as the series matures we see how Gon begins to show his dark side more and more, until his psychological deterioration culminates in the Chimera Ant arc, specifically with Pitou and the death of Kite. Gon is easy to explain, since his techniques are quite basic for Nen techniques, and most of his combat focuses on his superhuman abilities of perception, endurance, strength, and speed, which allowed him to reach very high levels even before acquiring his Nen powers.

Gon possesses all the benefits granted by his status as a Hunter. At the start of the series, he had a fishing rod that he could wield with disconcerting precision, even managing to snatch Hisoka's tag from afar while the latter was moving at great speed. The fishing rod can also be used as an offensive weapon if the situation requires it. During the Hunter Exam, Gon was frequently praised for his observation skills, agility, and endurance. In battle, he thinks quickly to come up with alternative solutions and strategies. However, his most notable quality is the hard-to-measure and almost limitless potential with which he is gifted. Many powerful characters such as Hisoka, Wing, Biscuit, and Razor were astonished when they grasped the true magnitude of Gon's talent, which is often described as limitless. This allows him to learn extremely fast, especially in the field of Nen, where an aptitude like his is very rare. Even before learning it, he managed to use Zetsu on one occasion.

Along with his potential, it is his mental strength that continually leaves more experienced and skilled fighters perplexed, and it is a crucial component of his overflowing charisma, which draws even more powerful Hunters toward him. His indomitable will allows him to continually put his life on the line without being held back by fear, which in turn allows him to make decisions that others would never dare to make. Gon prefers to attack rather than defend, even sacrificing himself to deal damage, since his stubbornness tends to come out during combat. Because of this, he sometimes throws himself in recklessly, but at the same time he does not give up until he can no longer move. He is quite competent in hand-to-hand combat after being trained in the fundamentals of Shingen-ryu kung fu by Wing and Biscuit, as well as through natural experience. Although this quality often goes unnoticed, Gon can concentrate intensely even in the most difficult circumstances, noticing details that even Single-Star Hunters would overlook.

Other abilities he has include sleeping with a larger part of his brain awake than normal, thanks to Biscuit's training, and holding his breath for several minutes even when fatigued. After leaving Greed Island, due to his prowess in Nen, Gon became able to blend in with nature more easily, which allowed him to discover multiple new animal species at a pace that astonished Kite's companions.

Gon is an Enhancer and is therefore gifted with great strength and recovery abilities. Thanks to Biscuit's training, he has trained in at least two additional types of Nen, Emission and Transmutation, which he later refined enough to create functional Nen abilities. Originally, he displayed greater skill in Emission than in Transmutation, but by the time he met Kite in the former NGL Zone, he may have become more proficient in the latter category than in the former, as the combat potential of his Nen abilities suggests. Gon has also mastered the four basic techniques and at least five of the advanced ones (the possible exceptions being In and En, although he can use Ren to detect attacks he would otherwise be unable to react to). According to Wing, Gon's talent is found in only one out of every ten million people. He successfully performed Ten with barely any instruction, a feat Wing described as both "amazing" and "terrifying," and he learned Ren and Gyo in just one night. Under normal conditions, he was able to maintain Ren for three hours outside of combat without the slightest difficulty. With this technique, he could pulverize aura-strengthened objects thrown at him by a fellow Enhancer even in the early stages of his training. While trying to develop a Nen ability, he discovered the advanced technique Ko on his own after Wing suggested he show Tsezguerra everything he had learned at the same time, and he can deploy it quickly and in multiple parts of the body simultaneously. Likewise, he learned to use Shu simply by imagining the technique, and he succeeded on the first attempt. This means that, along with Zetsu, which he managed to use even before being introduced to the concept of Nen, this achievement qualifies him as a genius at Nen. Gon was able to discover three of the eleven techniques on his own. Although he was strictly a novice in that respect, he had some basic skill in reading an opponent's aura flow and estimating the amount deployed.

This, of course, allowed him to create his own Nen technique, the Jajanken, named after a game he plays based on the Japanese word for rock, paper, and scissors, "Janken," and the word "Jajan," or Ta da! Gon came up with the idea after learning the dangerous roots of the game within the martial arts. Jajanken consists of three parts. Gon's main attack is Rock, a simple but devastating blow that concentrates all his aura into his fist through Ko. His long-range attack is Paper, an emitted attack that fires a ball of aura from his palm; this used to be his weakest attack, the ball being somewhat slow, not very powerful, and easy to block or redirect for anyone using Ko. His mid-range attack is Scissors, a transmuted blade of aura attached to his index and middle fingers, capable of cutting through a Chimera Ant. Gon preceded each attack with the chant "First comes the rock! Rock, paper...", followed by the name of the attack. Since all three attacks were charged in the same way, Gon could decide to switch from one to another even after the preparations had begun, adapting if the opponent moved closer or backed away. Later, he became able to charge Jajanken even while running.

The long charge time made Jajanken difficult to use in real combat, although the risks it posed were the core of its power. However, through a technique called second round or Akio de, Gon is able to gather twice the normal amount of aura to use two Jajanken attacks in rapid succession, eliminating the need to perform the chant twice. The name of this technique was shouted after the first attack and immediately before the second. It is currently unclear whether Akio de was a different Nen ability or simply part of the Jajanken.

After the siege of the palace in the Chimera Ant arc, through a Contract, Gon sacrificed all of his innate talents to obtain immense power, with which he managed to defeat one of the Royal Guards, Neferpitou, on his own. He used this ability without any prior training; the only knowledge he possessed about Vows at that time came from Kurapika's explanation of his Nen ability. However, as a result, his body began to deteriorate shortly after the fight, and even after being restored to full health, Gon lost the ability to use or perceive his aura. Ging cryptically stated that Gon had only gone back to being "normal" and should consider himself fortunate, since he could have lost much more. It is currently unknown whether he can reacquire his Nen, putting an end, for the time being, to Gon's Nen abilities.

As you can see, despite the fact that Gon's technique can be summed up as a game of rock, paper, and scissors with different effects, the mechanics by which the technique works are quite complex and are rooted in how diverse and strong Nen is as a power system, so it is exactly what we needed to begin this discussion about Nen techniques. It is just a shame that we probably won't see him reach levels similar to what he showed us that fateful night with Pitou in the future.
