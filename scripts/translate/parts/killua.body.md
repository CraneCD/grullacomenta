Every series worth its salt has those characters who are not merely supporting players helping to round out the protagonist, but who in many moments feel like protagonists themselves, being an essential part of the story and evolving just as much as, or even more than, their protagonist counterparts. These kinds of characters tend to be my favorites, and Hunter x Hunter gives us the best example of this with our little assassin from the Zoldyck family, Killua.

The key theme throughout Killua's entire character arc is the psychological concept of nature vs nurture. Nature vs nurture is a simple idea, but it has been an important topic of conversation in society ever since the question first arose. Nature has to do with genetics. It poses the question: are all humans and their behaviors a product of their DNA? Nurture has to do with the environment a person has had to live in. The nurture side asks: are all humans a byproduct of their surroundings and of the people they grew up with? Ultimately, the theory seeks to answer the question of how much control people have over their lives.

In Killua's case, understanding this concept is key, since it is the essence that drives his story. Killua is the heir of the Zoldyck family, a family of elite assassins who have been training him to be the head of the family since his birth. From the moment he drew his first breath, Killua has been subjected to torture, sent on missions to assassinate people, and forced to play psychological games with every member of his family. Being the head of the family is the only reason for his existence in the eyes of his family.

At the same time, Killua is also just a kid trying to find his own way in life. He was born with a good heart and is a fiercely loyal friend to Gon and to anyone else he considers a friend. Time and again, he proves to be a compassionate person who wants the best for those he cares about. Despite his own struggles, he has the endless motivation to be more than what others say he is.

The friendship between Gon and Killua is of the utmost importance to the development of both characters. Their mutual influence is tangible, especially as the show moves toward its final arcs. When the pair first meet during the Hunter Exam arc and up through the Zoldyck Family mini-arc, Killua simply wants to be Gon's friend. This is where the first great moment of his nature pushing back against his environment occurs. Killua hears that his friends have come for him while he is being tortured, and he frees himself with ease. He packs his things and heads to his father's room to tell him he is leaving. Surprisingly, he receives his father's blessing, and with that, he takes an important step on his path of personal growth.

The seed of his greatest internal conflict, that of his self-esteem, was planted in his childhood and slowly began to manifest during his time with Gon in the Yorknew City and the Greed Island isekai arcs. For example, at the end of the Greed Island arc, when Gon thanks him for always being there for him, Killua gets shy and turns away, thinking to himself that the roles should be reversed. Even though he has put a great deal of effort into the friendship, he still sees himself as being indebted to Gon.

In terms of power level and combat, Killua has been trained since birth to become a human weapon and is recognized by his family as the most talented assassin in the entire history of the Zoldycks. When he took the Hunter Exam for the first time, he was the fourth most powerful applicant (after Gittarackur, and, by his own admission, Hisoka and Hanzo). Up until the Hunter Exam, he wouldn't hesitate for a second to kill, but after meeting Gon this tendency diminished; even so, he is not afraid to resort to extreme means when circumstances demand it. Like his friend Gon, Killua has an astonishing natural talent that allows him to learn at a rapid pace and, supposedly, ad infinitum, a trait that sparked Hisoka's interest. His potential extends to Nen as well, as pointed out by Wing and Bisky. Although he fights mainly with his bare hands, Killua can use any kind of weapon, but the only one he consistently employs are his custom-made yo-yos. When he tried to prevent the Selection, he was so confident in his strength that he claimed tanks and fighter jets would be useless against him, and indeed, he was able to effortlessly subdue a military unit made up of at least four tanks.

His training granted him near immunity to poison, a high tolerance for electricity, great agility and physical strength, advanced armed and unarmed combat techniques, and stealth techniques, all of which he acquired at a very young age. He can also drive vehicles of any kind, including aircraft. His exceptional intellect and analytical skills are equally remarkable, and it is often through strategy that he can defeat opponents who are stronger or more experienced than he is. He is also capable of walking without making a sound and sleeping with a larger portion of his brain awake than normal, so that he can avoid attacks even while asleep, even if they come from a fellow armed, though weaker, Hunter. After leaving Greed Island, due to his prowess in Nen, Killua became able to blend in with nature more easily.

Killua's main weakness used to be an inferiority complex that paralyzed him with fear and prevented him from fighting opponents who could pose a threat to him, even if their overall strength was less than his own. Although it was originally believed to be a product of his training, it is later revealed that it was actually due to a Nen-imbued needle that Illumi had implanted in his brain. Its removal freed Killua from his brother's conditioning, thus regaining full control of his mental faculties in battle. This also had the effect of improving his overall movements, increasing his speed.

When it comes to his Nen abilities, Killua is a Transmuter with a dual affinity toward Enhancement. Killua is most efficient at changing the properties and shape of his aura. Like Gon, Killua's talent is said to be absolutely astonishing, found in only one out of every ten million people. Thanks to Bisky's training, he has refined his skills in at least two types of Nen besides his own, namely Enhancement and Conjuration. He has also mastered the four basic techniques and at least five of the advanced ones (the exceptions possibly being In and En, though he can extend his Ren to a radius of 57 cm to detect incoming attacks). He performed Ten successfully on the first try with almost no instruction, and learned Ren and Gyo in just one night. He was also able to perform Zetsu with great skill and without guidance after watching Gon use it against Gido, earning him praise from the Phantom Troupe, though they were able to detect him. He can maintain Ren for three hours outside of combat without the slightest difficulty, and Ten at all times. He can form Ko in an instant, as demonstrated when he used it to knock out Gon while the latter was going to fight Neferpitou. Although Gon can release a greater amount of aura, Killua is generally hailed as the one with the better control, to the point that when he faced Razor he was already able to distribute his aura with a margin of error of less than 1%, which led Bisky to comment that she only reached his degree of mastery in her twenties. Despite his lack of experience, he can accurately gauge how much aura to employ and in which area in a fraction of a second, relying solely on the instinctive knowledge granted by his fearsome potential. He easily surpassed Gon during their training on Greed Island, overcoming his inexperience with pure intuition.

Killua developed a series of Nen abilities in which he alters the properties of his aura to make it similar to electricity. This was inspired and made possible by the years of electric shocks he received as a child, both as training against torture and as punishment. Bisky was very impressed that he could create such a complex Nen ability at his age. He can electrocute his opponent either through physical contact or while keeping his distance, channeling his aura through his yo-yos or launching it in the form of a bolt. Although most Nen users take no damage from the electricity itself, they are still wounded and paralyzed for a few moments.

Killua has a series of special techniques that he employs through his electricity. These are:

Lightning Palm:

This is the most basic of Killua's Nen abilities, with which he uses his palms to electrocute his opponent with a shove. This paralyzes the opponent for a few seconds, allowing Killua or his allies to attack or escape.

Thunderbolt:

With this technique Killua jumps into the air and fires a bolt from his fingers, paralyzing the opponent and causing a good amount of damage. Although its power was not able to finish off even Rammot, we saw how it was strong enough to incapacitate Youpi for a few moments.

Godspeed

The first main function of this technique allows Killua to react automatically to external stimuli. Instead of the normal process in which the stimulus is perceived by the nerves, which transmit a signal to the brain, which in turn sends an instruction to the muscles, Killua programs his aura to send an electrical signal to his muscles to react instantly to a threat, thus bypassing the nervous system, which is comparatively slower. The aura can be preprogrammed to respond to different stimuli: physical contact, malevolent intent in the enemy's aura, or someone stepping into a predetermined perimeter, and the specific response is also preprogrammed.

Specifically, this results in a drastic increase in Killua's reaction time, which has both offensive and defensive uses. When Killua used a prototype of this ability, he was able to grab a dart the instant it was conjured, already in contact with his skin, and the moment it pierced him. The Whirlwind, as part of this ability is called, is also effective as an offensive ability. If the opponent executes the action for which Killua has preprogrammed a response, in most cases he will be able to attack before the opponent does, thanks to the fact that both his reaction time and his movement speed are drastically improved. When he used this application of Godspeed against Youpi, Killua was able to momentarily disappear from his sight and strike him several times without him being able to do anything, the brief lapse between the increase of the Royal Guard's aura and his action being enough for Killua to anticipate any move from his adversary. Youpi himself admitted to feeling completely helpless during the beating. On that occasion, even though his attacks inflicted no damage, Killua's electricity was powerful enough to provoke cries of pain from Youpi, as well as to paralyze him for a few moments. When he later used the same ability against Shaiapouf's clone, which is considerably fast itself, Killua was able to dodge its attacks with Komugi on his back, which led Pouf to fear that even with all of his cells, he would have difficulty catching him, and then he burned his cells by generating an electric current between his fingers each time the clone stepped on a circle he had drawn.

Since giving an order to one's aura is generally a Manipulation ability, it is possible, though unconfirmed, that Killua's Whirlwind uses this category of Nen.

The second application of Godspeed allows Killua to maintain conscious control of his movements. His running speed and jumping ability increase drastically, so he uses it mainly to cover long distances in very little time. As a consequence, this main function is more affected than the Whirlwind by the terrain: Tsubone could keep Killua in sight while he ran through the forest, but lost sight of him as soon as he reached a paved road. However, even at full speed, he was significantly outpaced by Tsubone's motorcycle transformation while she carried two passengers. The butler estimated that crossing the forest would take Killua about 10 minutes to reach a destination some 40 km away, while a car driver would take twice as long. This suggests that on unfavorable terrain, Killua's speed with his Godspeed is approximately 240 km/h.

As you can see, Killua's ability, although it appears to be quite simple, is supremely complex when we analyze how it works in its application of Nen, which perfectly reflects his development as a character. Personally, it is one of my favorite abilities, both for its use in battle and for how it reflects Killua's past and his way of fighting as an assassin.
