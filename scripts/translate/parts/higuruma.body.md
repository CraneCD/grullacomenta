Something we don't see much of in Jujutsu Kaisen is those characters who are completely disillusioned with the reality of the world, and how this entirely shapes their personality and motivations. A clear example is Geto, but aside from him few characters have shown us this kind of change. Recently, however, Hiromi Higuruma has shown us what it means to go from being an idealist to completely losing faith in the system he is part of, and his story reveals the complexity that exists even within the most upright of men when they are pushed to their moral limits, while his cursed techniques are an excellent thematic complement to his character.

Higuruma is one of the most complex characters in the entire series. As a defense attorney, Higuruma was renowned for his extraordinary intellect and his willingness to take on impossible cases in order to defend the unjustly accused. He strove to uphold the ideal that everyone deserves a fair trial under the law. However, when the justice system failed one of Higuruma's clients in a way that deeply shook his beliefs, it triggered changes within the lawyer.

Born in Tokyo, Higuruma began his career as a defense attorney, specializing in impossible cases where his clients were almost certain to be found guilty. Those closest to him considered him a bit of a masochist for taking on such difficult trials, but it was his passion for justice that drove him. With an extraordinarily brilliant mind that earned him praise even in the rigorous world of law school, Higuruma truly believed in protecting the innocent and giving even the accused a chance to prove reasonable doubt.

Over time, the nature of the work and the constant exposure to unjust verdicts took a toll on his mental state. Although he always tried to keep cases from affecting him personally, the expressions of horror on his clients' faces when they were found guilty despite overwhelming evidence in their favor haunted Higuruma. It became too much to ignore after his cursed techniques abruptly manifested during a trial in which clear evidence proved his client's innocence, yet they were declared guilty all the same. This pivotal moment marked a turning point that led Higuruma down a much darker path.

When his most recent client was found guilty despite the overwhelming evidence in their favor, it represented a failure of the justice system that Higuruma had devoted his career to defending. Still trying to come to terms with his newfound jujutsu abilities, Hiromi reacted with anger and pain, attacking the prosecutor and the judge in a moment of rage. This tragic event weighed heavily on him, as taking a life went against his moral code. It marked a shift in his perspective, leaving him disillusioned with the laws of man and disconnected from his former role as a defense attorney. He became generally discontent and detached from society, claiming that he now cared little for anything.

It was during the Culling Game, however, that Higuruma's character underwent another change. Initially viewing it as a way to simply observe how the rules might unfold without restrictions, he encountered Yuji Itadori and rejected his request for points to help stop the game. This set them on a collision course as antagonists. However, Yuji's willingness to confess to a crime he was not guilty of resonated with Higuruma's sense of justice, reminding him of why he became a lawyer in the first place.

With Yuji's innocence mirroring that of his client, who had faced unjust punishment, the former defense attorney had a change of heart. He reoriented himself to once again prioritize protecting the weak. Higuruma's cooperation with Yuji marked an intriguing change that showcased the complexity within this multifaceted Jujutsu Kaisen character.

Upon entering the arena of the Culling Game, Higuruma quickly distinguished himself as one of the best players. With barely any jujutsu experience aside from what he acquired in the short time since his awakening, Higuruma reached a level comparable to that of Grade 1 sorcerers in just twelve days. He exorcised numerous curses and killed at least twenty opponents within the colony.

It was there that he first crossed paths with Yuji. After Higuruma had Judgeman confiscate Yuji's cursed energy and perceived his disadvantage, the powerful sorcerer attacked with lethal intent. Yuji only managed to save himself by demanding a retrial, which halted their battle. This dangerous situation showcased Higuruma's combat prowess and his willingness to eliminate any threat.

From that moment on, Higuruma cooperated with Yuji's goal of stopping the Culling Game, even using his accumulated points to help save trapped civilians. Although they briefly parted ways to atone for their past crimes, Higuruma proved to be a valuable, if unstable, ally with potentially world-class abilities. His interactions with Yuji revealed new depths to this complex character in the series.

At the core of Hiromi's abilities lie his tremendous cursed energy and sharp intellectual talents, having unlocked his powers through natural talent and rapid self-teaching.

His cursed technique is called Judgeman, and it is thematically appropriate given his former profession. Using his vast amount of cursed energy, Higuruma summons a single Shikigami with a terrifying appearance, clearly based on a macabre version of the personification of the law. This shikigami features a set of scales, through which it can measure the opponent's guilt in an arbitrary crime that Higuruma decides to accuse them of. On its own, this technique does not seem to have much practical use; however, in a rather curious case, Higuruma appears to have awakened his domain expansion simultaneously with his cursed technique, and it is this domain that makes his technique and shikigami extremely terrifying.

Within his domain expansion, Deadly Sentence, Hiromi summons his judge shikigami. The domain takes the form of a circular courtroom, with guillotines surrounding a central stage. As the "judge," Hiromi interrogates his opponents while Judgeman observes passively, its verdicts influencing the conditions within the battle. Once an opponent is "convicted," Hiromi gains an advantage by increasing the size of his cursed weapon, the judge's gavel, which he wields with expert martial skill.

This domain is unusual in that it does not contain a guaranteed hit; instead, those trapped within it must follow the domain's rules, which include a complete prohibition of violence inside it. The domain includes a binding vow that requires Higuruma to explain the rules of the trial. First, Judgeman announces the charges against the accused, which can arbitrarily be things they may or may not have done; of course, Judgeman is relatively omnipotent within the domain and already knows whether the accused is guilty or not.

Higuruma will then lay out his case for why his opponent is guilty, and the accused will have the options of remaining silent, confessing, or denying the accusation and presenting their arguments. Should they choose the last option, they will not be able to lie. There is also the complication of an envelope containing additional evidence, which is only revealed at the end of the trial and may change Judgeman's verdict. In the end, if the accused is found guilty, they will then be punished with one of multiple sentences according to the severity of the case. These range from losing all of their cursed energy to the death sentence, which takes the form of a sword of light that will kill anyone it comes into contact with. It is important to mention that if the case is serious enough, both sentences can be handed down at once.

A curious case is that the accused can request a retrial if they believe the verdict was unjust and was made without all the available evidence, which, for example, allowed Yuji to use this for a retrial with Sukuna as co-defendant.

What truly sets Higuruma apart is his genius-level intelligence and his ability to achieve mastery of jujutsu in just two weeks, including barrier techniques. This rapid self-teaching allowed him to develop his own unique domain expansion, demonstrating his immense potential and love for the law.

Throughout the world of Jujutsu Kaisen, Higuruma emerges as one of the most fascinating characters in the series. Starting out as a passionate defender of justice who later becomes disillusioned, he undergoes compelling changes in ideology and loyalty. Although volatile after being betrayed by the justice system he had dedicated his career to defending, Higuruma demonstrates nuanced layers beneath an initial antagonism.

His change of heart and cooperation with Yuji reveal deeper principles of protecting the vulnerable, which first awakened jujutsu in Higuruma. Powerful yet intelligent, threatening yet vulnerable, Higuruma intrigues through a complex humanity. He embodies how even the most disheartened can find purpose and redemption. In a story that celebrates both the shadow and the light of humanity, Hiromi Higuruma stands out profoundly as one of the greatest achievements in character development in Jujutsu Kaisen.
