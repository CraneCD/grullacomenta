There are antagonists who, if you give the story a little twist, become the protagonists. People whose methods or motivations may seem twisted or evil, but who, once you understand their perspective, you start to see in another light, and you may even grow fond of them or identify with them. Hunter X Hunter has shown us several examples of this type of character, and in my opinion some of its best executions, with Chrollo Lucilfer, the leader of the Phantom Troupe and Kurapika's archenemy, being the clearest of them all.

Chrollo is calm, intelligent and charismatic, since he seems to be a born leader, capable of keeping the Troupe together despite the volatile personalities of its members. Although he is a highly capable strategist, Chrollo also has great confidence in his own fighting abilities, as well as in those of the other members of the Spiders. He always has at least two people with him, which is why Hisoka found it so difficult to fight him. After admiring the objects he has targeted and acquired, Chrollo sells them all. It is also said that Chrollo likes old books.

Unshakable and unflappable, he is also an observer of human nature. Gon's hostility toward the Troupe led Chrollo to reflect on the nature of himself and his actions. Furthermore, while fighting Hisoka, who refused to back down even after seeing the magnitude of his abilities, Chrollo expressed a fascination with the inner workings of human beings. However, by his own admission, he sees no difference between a puppet and a person.

Chrollo shows no fear of his own death; he even anticipates it. Melody stated that his heartbeat sounded as if he were happy living alongside death. For Chrollo, the Troupe is more important than his own life. He once stated that the survival of the group takes precedence over the well-being of the leader, emphasizing that the head of the Spiders is just another member who can be sacrificed for the survival of the whole. To prevent his misfortunes from coming true, Chrollo ordered the members of the Troupe to stay close and help one another. The only people Chrollo seems to care about are his fellow Troupe members. When he reads his fortune, written by Neon, which alludes to Uvogin's death, he weeps for him. Later, he organizes a massacre of Mafia members as a requiem for his fallen comrade. After the deaths of Kortopi and Shalnark at Hisoka's hands, Chrollo appears visibly distressed and furious, swearing to kill him as soon as he sees him.

Like the other members of the Troupe, Chrollo is ruthless, relentless and not above committing inhuman mass murders, most notably when they brutally slaughtered the Kurta Clan. Furthermore, his use of Indoor Fish on an assassin reflects his apathetic nature toward his enemies. The atrocious murder of his dear friend Sarasa and his thirst for revenge against the perpetrators have desensitized him to such atrocities. A hostility between Chrollo and Silva Zoldyck is also suggested, probably stemming from their earlier encounter after Silva killed a member of the Troupe.

Chrollo is a skilled and quite manipulative actor, as shown when he met Neon Nostrade and "befriended" her in order to steal her fortune-telling ability. During that time, he presented himself as a kind and helpful man, always enthusiastic and smiling, who also showed curiosity. However, Chrollo only intended to learn about Neon's ability in order to fulfill one of the conditions necessary to steal it. While kidnapped, Chrollo also proved to be quite provocative and sinister toward Kurapika.

It is said that the inhabitants of Meteor City also share notable bonds that have been described as "thinner than water, and yet thicker than blood." This was also demonstrated by the fact that the Phantom Troupe is an important asset in the city's defense.

Chrollo is shown to have been emotional, affectionate and bright as a child. He also always brought fresh flowers for the graves of the children at the church. Father Lisores remarks to an elder of Meteor City that he sees great potential in Chrollo due to his spirit and original ideas.

In terms of power level, as the leader of the Phantom Troupe, Chrollo is an exceptional fighter and is revered as the strongest by most of its members. Machi clearly ranks him above Hisoka, who has consistently wished to fight the Troupe's leader far more than any other Spider. However, Chrollo tends to hold back if his opponent possesses an ability that interests him so that he can steal it. He was able to fight Silva and Zeno Zoldyck simultaneously, both extremely competent assassins, without leaving any opening for the former to exploit, although he was unable to cause them significant damage and was ultimately saved by Illumi fulfilling his contract. In the past, he survived a confrontation with Silva, and Zeno stated that he would surely win if Chrollo fought to steal his ability, but that his victory would be less certain if Chrollo were serious about killing him. His greatest achievement on the battlefield may be his victory at Heavens Arena against Hisoka, another extremely powerful Nen user who at the time had no record of past defeats or even any evidence of having struggled in fights.

Chrollo's fighting style is adaptable and combines speed with deadly precision. He is capable of killing skilled assassins with just a pen, sometimes even remaining hidden with Zetsu, without using Nen at all as a result. Chrollo also possesses extraordinary strategic skills, as well as astonishing mental strength and willpower, even by the Troupe's standards.

Chrollo is in possession of one of the famous Ben's Knives, forged in the central period of Delon. It has a black handle with a slightly curved guard, similar to a D-guard. Near the guard, the blade splits into two segments. The central one is the longest and ends in a sharp, arrow-shaped point. The second segment is curved and serves as the knife's edge, with its tip almost touching one of the central segments. A third segment acts as the back of the blade, connected to the others through two horizontal bars. Ben's Knives are known for their exceptional sharpness, which allowed Chrollo to cut Silva's skin, a feat that led the assassin to recognize the quality of the weapon. Silva also deduced from the shape of the blade that it was poisonous. The poison was so potent that 0.1 mg could paralyze a whale. Chrollo kept it hidden in a curved sheath under his clothes.

As far as his Nen is concerned, Chrollo is a Specialist. His talent for Nen is considered prodigious not only for his age, but even by the standards of masters of the discipline. Silva pointed out that his Zetsu is impeccable, allowing him to sneak up on his opponent and kill them without leaving the slightest trace of bloodlust, although Hisoka was able to detect him in an instant on at least one occasion. Like other skilled Nen users, he is able to maintain a state of Ten at all times. His prolonged use of Ren in confrontations suggests that he can also use Ken. In addition, he has knowledge of Gyo. However, the technique he relies on most appears to be Hatsu, proving to be extremely versatile in his use of it. He has been able to employ numerous stolen abilities in battle with great effectiveness, despite the fact that they use different types of Nen, and he has even used multiple Nen abilities simultaneously, thus demonstrating his vast reserve of aura. It is currently unknown whether this is entirely due to his own abilities or whether Skill Hunter allows him to mimic the skill of the ability's original owner.

Although Chrollo ranked seventh in the Troupe in arm wrestling, this is impressive considering that Specialists are the weakest in the use of Enhancement abilities. Moreover, his defensive use of aura is so great that, despite fighting Silva and Zeno at the same time and withstanding powerful blows, he only suffered relatively minor damage that did not affect his combat abilities, although he immediately understood that he would not be able to defend himself against Zeno's superior Gyo and collapsed from exhaustion right after the fight.

Because of Kurapika's Chain Jail, he was left unable to use Nen for a time. After the exorcism was performed, he acquired new abilities for the purpose of fighting Hisoka. The creation of Double Face and his explanation of The Sun and Moon imply that he knows about legal loopholes and post-mortem Nen, respectively.

Let's talk a little about Chrollo's Hatsu, Skill Hunter, or Bandit's Secret

Chrollo can steal the Nen abilities of others to use as his own through a conjured book known as "Bandit's Secret." The victim, of course, no longer has access to the stolen ability. The theft occurs under strict conditions:

Chrollo must witness the Nen ability in action with his own eyes.
He must ask questions about the Nen and be answered by the victim.
The victim's palm must touch the handprint on the cover of Bandit's Secret.
All of the above must be completed within one hour.
To use a stolen ability, Chrollo first conjures Bandit's Secret and turns to the page of the desired ability. The book must remain open in his right hand, which allows him to use only one ability at a time. Abilities are subject not only to the limitations but also to the emotions and thoughts of their creators. For example, despite seeing corpses as motionless masses no different from objects, Chrollo cannot use Order Stamp on them due to the original owner's opinion. Furthermore, an ability disappears from the book if its former owner dies. However, if post-mortem Nen is involved, Chrollo can still use the ability. Expert Nen users can understand some of these conditions, like Zeno, who correctly guessed that Chrollo needed to fulfill four or five of them before being able to steal an ability.

Additionally, Chrollo can use Double Face, which allows him to maintain access to any of his stolen abilities on the page of his book. Even if the book is closed and he is not touching it, acting as an exception to one of the conditions of his main ability. With both hands free, Chrollo can fight hand to hand in conjunction with his stolen abilities. Furthermore, if Chrollo keeps the book open to a particular page and uses his bookmark on another page, he can use two stolen abilities at the same time. This bookmark seems to belong to the book itself, and not to be a stolen ability.

The ones that are stolen abilities are the following:

Indoor Fish:

These Nen Beasts, described as a fish that can only survive in a completely sealed room, appear to be made entirely of bones and feed on human flesh. While a person is being devoured, they feel no pain and do not bleed, and will remain alive and perfectly conscious even if mortally wounded. When the fish disappears, the effects of the wounds manifest, most likely resulting in the victim's death. Chrollo summoned two of them when fighting an assassin hired by the Ten Dons.

Fun Fun Cloth

An ability originally belonging to Owl, a member of the Shadow Beasts. The cloth is conjured and can increase and decrease in size according to the user's will. Anything covered with the cloth, even an object as large as a car, decreases in size until it fits in the palm of the hand. The user can then return it to its original size by taking it out of the bag. People can also be shrunk in this way, without suffering harm. After Owl used it to move the objects at the Underground Auction, he was eventually captured and tortured by the Troupe, during which Chrollo stole the ability. Chrollo then used this ability in his fight against Silva and Zeno, attempting to capture the latter so that he could steal his ability as well.

Teleportation:

Chrollo showed the ability to teleport a person from one place to another without that person's consent. He did so by remaining motionless and without seeing the person he wanted to teleport or the destination. He used this ability on Nobunaga to prevent him from attacking Hisoka. Both Phinks and Machi were unaware that Chrollo possessed this ability.

The Sun and Moon:


Two symbols appear on the backs of Chrollo's hands: a sun with a plus sign on his left hand and a waxing/waning crescent moon with a minus sign on his right hand. By touching the desired target, he can mark them with a different symbol depending on which hand he used. When the two symbols touch each other, they cause an explosion. The symbols can be created instantly, but to reach their maximum power, Chrollo needs to touch the target for three to five seconds. In the latter case, the explosion is powerful enough to kill the people around the bearer of the symbols, not just the bearer themselves. A symbol can be fully charged even after its application, through physical contact. Despite Chrollo claiming that the symbols themselves explode, fully charged symbols were able to turn whatever they were applied to into a bomb, even parts separated after the symbols were applied. The ability was stolen from the elder of Meteor City. Although he has since died, his Nen became so powerful after his death that Chrollo was able to retain his ability. For the same reason, once the symbols are applied, they will remain until they explode, even if Chrollo's book is closed. Their power is such that they can counteract Skill Hunter's restrictions, preventing a copy created with Gallery Fake from disappearing even after the ability is no longer in use, if a symbol has been applied to it. The Nen type of this ability is currently unknown, although its effects could be explained by a combination of Emission and Transmutation.

Order Stamp:

Chrollo conjures a stamp that, when applied to a "puppet," allows the user to control it after giving a vocal command. To be considered a puppet, the object the user wishes to control must have a head and be lifeless. This ability cannot be used to control corpses, since the original user did not see them as mere inanimate objects; however, it can control Nen copies of corpses. Removing the puppet's head will deactivate the ability. The stamp bears the kanji for "person" (人) and leaves the same mark when applied. The ability is capable of controlling a large number of puppets, but the orders cannot be too complex. Chrollo discovered that if he controls the copy of a person, giving orders that go against that person's beliefs can be detrimental, so much so that sometimes the order is ignored. However, he discovered that using language that creates emotional distance, such as saying "break" instead of "kill," can bypass the puppet's will. This is one of the abilities Chrollo stole to fight Hisoka, and it can be used to control more than 200 puppets at the same time.

Convert Hands

Two marks appear on the palms of Chrollo's hands: a black arrow pointing upward inside an empty circle on his right hand and a white arrow pointing downward inside a filled circle on his left hand. By touching a target with his right hand, it takes on Chrollo's appearance, while by touching it with his left hand, Chrollo takes on the target's appearance. If Chrollo touches a target with both hands, both instantly change form. The ability only affects appearances, not abilities or personalities. Since the marks remain on Chrollo's hands, it is relatively easy to identify him, although clothing can be used to conceal them. Nonetheless, it is an effective way to blend into a crowd. For the mark to appear on his right hand, which would otherwise hold the book, Chrollo needs to use the bookmark, but after activating the ability, he can hold the book normally. This is one of the abilities Chrollo stole to fight Hisoka. It is currently unknown what type of Nen it uses. Its characteristic of making the affected object indistinguishable from the original makes it vaguely similar to some Conjuration abilities.

Additionally, Chrollo has stolen abilities that he no longer possesses within his arsenal, such as Neon's Lovely Ghostwriter, Kortopi's Gallery Fake, or Shalnark's Black Voice. It seems that Chrollo can return the abilities he steals, or that they disappear upon the death of the original user and vanish from his book.

Chrollo is an interesting character. On the one hand, we know he is a psychopath who would do anything to protect or avenge his own, yet isn't this what the other protagonists of Hunter x Hunter do as well? It's just that Chrollo is a bit more brutal and realistic about it. Additionally, his abilities demonstrate the versatility of Nen, and thanks to him we learn more about many other Nen techniques in greater detail, which makes him deserving of his popularity.
