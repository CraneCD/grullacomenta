Master figures are essential to most Shonen. These characters are crucial for characterizing the protagonists, and through them a great deal of exposition is delivered about how the world of these series works. However, the potential of these characters to motivate or change the protagonists is rarely used to the fullest, given the close ties between them and their masters, and when this is executed perfectly, as Hunter x Hunter does with Kite and Gon, it becomes something unforgettable, and one of the most important pivot points we have seen in the world of Shonen. In this video, we will explore the character and Nen abilities of Kite, Gon's master figure, and try to understand why his death had the impact it did on our protagonist.

Kite is severe and stoic, occasionally blunt to the point of seeming rude. Although some may consider him cold, he is actually caring; he likes animals just as much as people. However, he never hesitates, not even when he must take a life, even if he dislikes it: in fact, after saving Gon, he struck him because his ignorance led to the death of a Foxbear.

It is clear how Kite has an extremely pronounced paternal side, and we could even say he is Gon's real father figure in addition to being his master, which explains the attachment the boy has to him. Ging's abandonment of Gon, and the time Gon spent in his childhood with Kite, marked him deeply, shaping his personality, which demonstrates Kite's ability to influence others.

What is interesting is that even after his death at the hands of Pitou, and the consequences this had for Gon, we see another side of Kite following his reincarnation as a Chimera Ant. During her initial introduction as the Queen's last offspring, Kite is presented as an enthusiastic little girl, still playing with stuffed toys and swords, and still being cared for by Colt. However, after only a few days, she matured and seemed to return to her former personality as a result of recovering her memories. She then helped both Koala and Gon to ease their feelings of guilt and gave the former a new purpose in life, although she framed it as a threat. This demonstrates not only her compassion and influence once again, but also her analytical skill and tactical intelligence.

In terms of power level, Kite is one of the strongest Hunters we know of, with extremely interesting abilities. He is not only an expert swordsman, as demonstrated by his skill with his Tachi, or elongated Katana, but he is also capable of fighting with a varied range of weapons, as demonstrated by his hatsu, which we will discuss shortly.

Kite, like Gon, is greatly appreciated by animals, a trait that, according to Ging, identifies a good Hunter. In the few years after passing the exam, he discovered 68 new species. One of his most impressive achievements, however, remains tracking down his master, the elusive Ging Freecss. His competence allowed him to mentor a group of amateur Hunters, as well as to guide novice Hunters like Gon and Killua. While they were in NGL, Killua observed that he and Gon together were weaker than a one-armed Kite. Kite could effortlessly defeat high-ranking Chimera Ants, organisms he had never encountered before, and even managed to inflict some superficial wounds on the newborn Neferpitou with only one arm, though at the cost of his life. When he was revived as a corpse, Shoot and Knuckle had to cooperate to defeat him and considered him a difficult adversary.

As for his Nen, Kite is a Conjurer with a dual affinity toward Transmutation. Given that he was trained in Nen by Ging, one of the best Nen users in the world, it can be assumed that Kite is highly proficient in this field. He is known to have a powerful aura. His En has a radius of about 45 meters, with fluctuations of 2 or 3 meters depending on his physical and mental condition, and he appears to be capable of masking it with In, since Gon and Killua were unable to notice that he was using the technique.

Kite's Hatsu is called Crazy Slots.

Kite can conjure a variety of weapons with a clown-shaped Nen beast attached to them. A slot machine appears on the clown's tongue with numbers from 1 to 9, each corresponding to a specific weapon. Whatever number appears randomly, the weapon corresponding to that number is conjured, which means that Kite cannot choose which weapon to summon. He included this restriction in order to increase the power of the individual weapons. The summoned weapon will not disappear until it has been used. The clown can also speak and comment on Kite's actions and conditions, usually to his annoyance. Only three of his weapons are shown in the manga: a scythe, a rifle, and a mace. Additionally, according to Ging, the Crazy Slots ability has a number that only appears when Kite desperately does not want to die. This ability is interesting because it demonstrates that his power is so great that Kite managed to reincarnate as the Queen's daughter after his death.

Let's talk about the known numbers:

Number 2: Silent Waltz

Kite conjures a very sharp and enormous scythe. It cannot be dismissed until Kite uses it against his opponent.

After increasing the size of the blade, Kite swings it in a circle, generating an invisible cut that spreads in all directions. When he used it against the Chimera Ants, he split them all in half and knocked down the trees around them.

The scythe is not one of Kite's preferred weapons, and he considers it a poor choice overall, which is strange considering his skill with the sword. However, even though it is a weapon he dislikes, he turns out to be extremely skilled and lethal with it.

Number 3: Mace

Kite conjures a mace or a staff with a clown's appearance, which he seems to wield with a reverse grip. The true orientation and function of this weapon are still largely unknown. However, the clown refers to it as a "good number," probably referring to its simplicity of use and attack power.

Number 4: Rifle

Kite conjures a firearm with a shape similar to a long-barreled pistol combined with an extremely long stock. It can fire powerful bullets in rapid succession and apparently without making any noise. The long front element of the weapon appears to be some kind of silencer, which explains the lack of noise when firing. This is one of Kite's few ranged weapons, and he appears to be just as skilled with it as with his close-range weapons.

We cannot forget the conditions for using his abilities: he cannot choose the weapon he is given, and the one that is summoned must be used before it can disappear. We can assume that these are his own conditions for strengthening the summoned weapons, in the same way that Kurapika did to strengthen his chains.

Sadly, we never learned the other numbers of Crazy Slots, so we do not know the full extent of Kite's hatsu or of his power level. Tell me in the comments which weapons you think would be associated with the other numbers.

It is confirmed that Kite lost his Nen abilities after his rebirth. However, it is not confirmed whether this changed his Aura type, whether his abilities can be relearned, or whether he is even capable of using Nen at all in his new body.
